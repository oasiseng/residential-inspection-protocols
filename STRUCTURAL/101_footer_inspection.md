# Protocol 101: Footer (Footing) Inspection — Pre-Pour Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Foundation
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 4 (Foundations)
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-pour verification of continuous spread footings for single-family residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after excavation and rebar placement but before concrete placement**.

### In Scope
- Footing dimensions (width, depth, bearing elevation).
- Longitudinal reinforcement (size, count, continuity, lap splices).
- Vertical dowels (size, spacing, hook orientation, projection height).
- Soil bearing conditions at the bottom of the excavation.
- Formwork or earth-formed footing conditions.
- Clear cover for reinforcement.

### Out of Scope
- Geotechnical bearing capacity testing (requires geotech report).
- Concrete mix design verification (batch tickets reviewed at pour).
- Post-pour honeycombing or consolidation checks.
- For Electrical bond beam inspection (see [Protocol 200](./200_footer_bond.md)).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the footing is ready for concrete placement by verifying:
- **PASS:** All dimensions, reinforcement, and soil conditions meet plans and FBC-R.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before pour authorization.
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring redesign or re-excavation.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Trench safety:** Do not enter excavations deeper than 4 ft without shoring or sloping per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P. Inspect from the edge where possible.
- **Do not** disturb placed reinforcement or move bars to "test" placement.
- **Do not** compact or probe bearing soil with tools that could disturb the bearing surface.
- **Stop condition:** If trench walls show active sloughing, groundwater intrusion, or evidence of fill material at bearing elevation — STOP and notify the contractor and geotechnical engineer before proceeding.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Excavation Dimensions

1. Measure footing **width** at a minimum of 3 locations along each run.
   - Typical residential: 16 in. to 24 in. wide (per plans).
   - Must meet or exceed plan dimension.
2. Measure footing **depth** from top of form (or ground surface) to bottom of excavation.
   - Minimum 6 in. thick for 1-story, 8 in. for 2-story (per FBC-R Table R403.1(1)).
   - Bottom of footing must be minimum 12 in. below undisturbed ground surface (FBC-R R403.1.1).
3. Check that the bottom of the trench is **level and on undisturbed native soil** (not backfill).
   - Look for color changes, loose material, organic matter, or debris.

### Step 2: Verify Soil Bearing Conditions

1. Visually confirm the bearing surface:
   - Soil should be firm, undisturbed, and free of standing water.
   - No organic material, loose fill, construction debris, or roots at bearing elevation.
2. If the geotechnical report specifies a required bearing stratum (e.g., limestone, compacted sand), confirm visual consistency.
3. **Red flag:** Soft/wet clay, peat, muck, or visible fill material at bearing elevation — HOLD for geotech review.

### Step 3: Verify Longitudinal Reinforcement (Bottom Steel)

1. Count the number of continuous longitudinal bars.
   - Typical: (2) #5 bars continuous (verify against plans).
2. Verify bar size using deformation pattern or bar markings.
   - #4 = 1/2 in. diameter, #5 = 5/8 in. diameter.
3. Check **lap splices**:
   - Minimum lap for #5 bars: typically 25 in. (verify per plans/ACI 318).
   - Splices must be tied with wire ties (not just overlapped loosely).
   - Stagger splices where possible (both bars should not splice at the same location).
4. Verify **clear cover**:
   - Minimum 3 in. from bottom and sides when cast directly against earth (ACI 318-19, 20.6.1.3).
   - Rebar must be supported on chairs, bricks, or dobies — NOT resting on the ground.
5. Check that bars are continuous around corners with proper hooks or bends per plans.

### Step 4: Verify Vertical Dowels (Stem Wall Connection)

1. Verify **bar size** of dowels (typically #5 per plans).
2. Verify **spacing** matches plans (e.g., 48 in. O.C., or as specified).
3. Measure **hook length** at bottom:
   - Standard 10 in. hook (per ACI 318 standard hook for #5 = 10 in. minimum).
   - Hook should be oriented to engage the longitudinal steel.
4. Verify **projection height** above top of footing:
   - Must provide minimum 25 in. lap splice with future stem wall vertical bars.
   - Bars must be plumb and properly positioned within the footing width.
5. Confirm vertical dowels are **tied to the longitudinal steel** — not free-floating.

### Step 5: Verify Rebar Support and Positioning

1. Confirm all reinforcement is supported at the correct elevation within the footing.
   - Bottom steel should be ~3 in. up from the bottom of the trench (on chairs/dobies).
2. Check that no reinforcement is in contact with the soil.
3. Confirm wire ties are tight and bars are stable — will not shift during the pour.
4. Look for any **unauthorized mechanical splices or clamp connections**:
   - Wire rope clamps (U-bolt clamps) on rebar are **NOT an approved splice method**.
   - Only lap splices, mechanical couplers listed in ACI 318, or welded splices (per AWS D1.4) are acceptable.

### Step 6: Check Utilities and Embeds

1. Verify any required sleeves or penetrations through the footing are placed per plans.
2. Confirm no underground utilities (water, sewer, electrical conduit) conflict with the footing location.
3. Check for any grounding electrode conductor (GEC) or Ufer ground requirements:
   - If required, verify rebar connection for concrete-encased electrode per NEC 250.52(A)(3).

### Step 7: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire footing perimeter and note any anomalies.
2. Photograph:
   - Overall footing layout (wide shot).
   - Longitudinal rebar with bar count visible.
   - Lap splice locations with measurement.
   - Vertical dowels with spacing measurement.
   - Soil bearing surface.
   - Any deficiencies or red flags.
3. Record all measurements on the field inspection form.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All dimensions meet plans, rebar count/size/spacing correct, cover adequate, soil bearing acceptable, dowels properly placed and tied |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable on-site (e.g., missing chair, one dowel out of position, minor trench wall slough not at bearing) |
| **FAIL** | Wrong bar size, missing longitudinal bars, inadequate cover, unauthorized splice method, footing on fill/disturbed soil, dimensions significantly under plan requirements |

---

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Ignores cast-against-earth cover requirements.** LLMs frequently cite 1.5 in. or 2 in. cover for footings. For concrete cast directly against earth, ACI 318 requires **3 in. minimum**. This is the most common AI error for footings.

2. **Conflates footing and slab reinforcement.** AI often recommends welded wire fabric (WWF) for footings. Footings require deformed reinforcing bars per the structural plans — WWF is for slabs-on-grade.

3. **Recommends "testing" rebar by bending or pulling.** Destructive field testing of placed reinforcement is not an inspection method. Visual verification of bar markings, size, and placement is the standard.

4. **Ignores Florida-specific wind and exposure requirements.** Generic AI responses default to IRC minimums without accounting for FBC high-velocity hurricane zone (HVHZ) requirements or exposure category, which can significantly increase footing size and reinforcement.

5. **Treats wire rope clamps as acceptable rebar splices.** U-bolt or cable clamps clamped onto rebar are NOT recognized by ACI 318 as a mechanical splice. This is a critical deficiency if found on site.

## 10) Documentation Minimum

- [ ] Project address, permit number, date, time, weather conditions.
- [ ] Name and PE license number of inspecting engineer.
- [ ] Plan sheet reference (foundation plan revision and date).
- [ ] Footing width and depth measurements (minimum 3 points per run).
- [ ] Longitudinal bar count, size, and lap splice lengths.
- [ ] Vertical dowel size, spacing, hook length, and projection height.
- [ ] Soil bearing condition notes (visual description).
- [ ] Clear cover verification.
- [ ] Photo set with scale reference (minimum 10 photos per inspection).
- [ ] Deficiency list (if any) with required corrective actions.
- [ ] Final disposition: PASS / HOLD / FAIL with PE signature.
- [ ] Limitations statement: "This inspection is limited to visual observation of accessible conditions at the time of inspection. Concealed conditions are excluded."

---

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Fill material or disturbed soil at bearing elevation | Hold pour → notify geotech for bearing evaluation |
| Groundwater at bearing elevation | Hold pour → evaluate dewatering or design modification |
| Rebar size/count does not match sealed plans | Hold pour → notify EOR (Engineer of Record) for resolution |
| Unauthorized splice method (clamps, etc.) | Fail → require removal and proper lap splice |
| Footing dimensions significantly under plan | Fail → notify EOR for redesign evaluation |
| Active trench wall collapse at bearing | Stop work → OSHA and geotech involvement |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Wide shot** — entire footing run showing excavation and surrounding site.
2. **Longitudinal steel** — close-up showing bar count, deformation pattern, and continuity.
3. **Lap splice** — tape measure showing splice length alongside the bars.
4. **Vertical dowels** — close-up showing hook at bottom and projection above footing.
5. **Dowel spacing** — tape measure between consecutive vertical dowels.
6. **Chair/support** — rebar support keeping steel off the ground.
7. **Bearing surface** — bottom of trench showing soil condition.
8. **Corner detail** — how longitudinal bars turn corners (hooks/bends).
9. **Any deficiencies** — close-up of any issues found with clear identification.
10. **Overview with context** — property, permit board, or address marker in frame.
