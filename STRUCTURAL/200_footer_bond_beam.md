# Protocol 200: Footer Bond Beam Inspection — Pre-Pour Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Foundation (Bond Beam / Stem Wall Base)
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 4 & 6
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-grout/pre-pour verification of the bond beam at the base of CMU (concrete masonry unit) stem walls in residential foundation systems. This protocol covers the inspection window **after the first course(s) of CMU block are laid on the footing and the bond beam reinforcement is placed, but before grouting**.

This is the critical connection between the poured footing and the masonry stem wall — where the vertical dowels from the footing lap with the vertical wall reinforcement, and the horizontal bond beam ties it all together.

### In Scope
- Bond beam block placement (knockout or lintel block) at correct course.
- Horizontal bond beam reinforcement (bar size, continuity, splices).
- Vertical rebar continuity from footing dowels through stem wall.
- Lap splice lengths (vertical bars: dowel to wall bar).
- Grout cell fill requirements.
- Clean-out and debris in cells.
- Connection to the porch/slab elevation (Section C details).

### Out of Scope
- Footing inspection (see [Protocol 101](./101_footer_inspection.md)).
- Above-grade wall framing or shearwall inspection.
- Stucco or exterior finish application.
- Slab-on-grade inspection (future protocol).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the bond beam and stem wall base are ready for grouting/pouring by verifying:
- **PASS:** Block courses, reinforcement, lap splices, and grout cells meet plans and FBC-R.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before grout authorization.
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies in reinforcement continuity or structural connections.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Do not** remove or shift laid block to inspect behind walls.
- **Do not** apply lateral force to test wall stability before grouting.
- **Do not** step on or stand on ungrouted block walls.
- Inspect from accessible sides only; use mirror/flashlight for cell interiors.
- **Stop condition:** If vertical reinforcement is missing or cannot be verified as continuous from footing to bond beam, STOP and notify the EOR.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Block Layout and Coursing

1. Confirm the block type and size:
   - Standard: 8 in. x 8 in. x 16 in. CMU (per plans).
   - Verify loadbearing block is used (not partition block).
2. Count block courses from top of footing to bond beam:
   - Typical: 3 block courses (24 in.) per Section C detail.
   - Verify against the plan elevation callout (e.g., 18.39 in. NAVD88 porch floor elevation).
3. Verify the bond beam course uses **knockout block (bond beam block)**:
   - The web knockouts must be removed to create a continuous horizontal channel.
   - Confirm the channel is clear for horizontal rebar placement.
4. Check that the block is **level and plumb**:
   - Mortar joints should be consistent (typically 3/8 in.).
   - No racked or tilted courses.

### Step 2: Verify Vertical Reinforcement Continuity

1. Confirm **vertical dowels from the footing** extend up through the block cells:
   - These are the #5 dowels placed during the footing pour (Protocol 101, Step 4).
   - They must project high enough to provide the required lap splice.
2. Verify **vertical wall reinforcement** is placed in the cells:
   - Typically #5 vertical bars per plans.
   - Check spacing matches plans (see plans for O.C. spacing).
3. **Measure the lap splice** between the footing dowel and the vertical wall bar:
   - Minimum lap: **25 in.** for #5 bars (per plans and ACI 318).
   - Both bars must be in the same cell with adequate overlap.
   - Bars must be **tied** at the lap — not just loosely adjacent.
4. Verify the vertical bar is **centered in the cell** and not pressed against the block face:
   - Minimum cover through grout to face of block: 1.5 in. for masonry.

### Step 3: Verify Horizontal Bond Beam Reinforcement

1. Confirm **bar size**: typically (1) #5 bar continuous in the bond beam (per Section C detail).
2. Verify the bar is **continuous** around the perimeter:
   - Check lap splices in the horizontal bar: minimum 25 in. lap.
   - Splices should be staggered and not located at corners.
3. Verify the bar sits in the **bottom of the bond beam channel**:
   - Minimum 1.5 in. cover (top and bottom within grout-filled cell).
4. Confirm the horizontal bar is **tied to the vertical reinforcement** at intersections.

### Step 4: Verify Grout Cell Conditions

1. Inspect each cell that contains reinforcement:
   - Cells must be **clean** — free of mortar droppings, debris, dirt, and standing water.
   - Use a flashlight and mirror to check the bottom of each cell.
2. Verify that **all cells with reinforcement will be grouted**:
   - Per FBC-R R606.12, all cells containing reinforcement must be grouted solid.
3. Confirm grout pour height does not exceed maximum lift height:
   - Typical maximum: 4 ft pour lift for fine grout, 8 ft for coarse grout with adequate consolidation.

### Step 5: Verify Section-Specific Details

Reference the plan section details (A, B, C) for the specific condition being inspected:

#### Section A — Standard Interior Footing-to-Wall
- (2) #5 bars continuous in footing.
- #5 dowel with standard 10 in. hook at bottom, projecting up.
- 8 in. x 8 in. x 16 in. CMU block.
- 4 in. concrete slab with 6x6 W1.4xW1.4 welded wire fabric or fiber mesh.
- Vapor barrier below slab on compacted fill.

#### Section B — Exterior or Shearwall Condition
- Same footing reinforcement as Section A.
- **Horizontal shearwall reinforcement** in the wall (per shearwall schedule).
- Header block at top of stem wall (typical).
- Wood base board connection at slab level.

#### Section C — Porch / Elevated Slab Condition
- 8 in. x 8 in. knockout block bond beam, filled at 3 block courses.
- 3000 PSI grout fill with (1) #5 bar continuous in bond beam.
- 1.5 in. minimum top cover and 25 in. lap splice for horizontal bar.
- #5 vertical rebar with minimum 25 in. lap.
- #5 dowels at 24 in. x 24 in. grid (porch slab support).
- 36 in. O.C. vapor barrier below porch floor.
- Proposed grade and finished floor elevation per plans (18.39 in. NAVD88).

### Step 6: Check for Common Field Errors

Walk the entire stem wall and specifically look for:

1. **Missing vertical bars** — cells without required reinforcement.
2. **Short lap splices** — dowel projection insufficient for 25 in. minimum.
3. **Unauthorized splices** — wire rope clamps, hose clamps, or other non-approved mechanical connections on rebar (see close-up photo reference).
4. **Mortar-clogged cells** — mortar droppings blocking grout flow around rebar.
5. **Wrong block type** — solid or partition block used instead of knockout/bond beam block.
6. **Missing knockout removal** — bond beam block webs not knocked out, preventing continuous horizontal bar placement.
7. **Displaced vertical bars** — bars pushed to one side of the cell, reducing cover.

### Step 7: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire stem wall perimeter and note any anomalies.
2. Photograph:
   - Overall stem wall layout.
   - Bond beam block course (showing knockout channel).
   - Horizontal bond beam bar with measurement.
   - Vertical rebar lap splice with tape measure.
   - Cell interiors (clean and clear).
   - Any deficiencies found.
3. Record all measurements on the field inspection form.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All block courses correct, bond beam bar size/continuity verified, vertical lap splices meet 25 in. minimum, cells clean and ready for grout, all section details match plans |
| **HOLD** | Minor debris in cells (cleanable), one bar slightly mispositioned but correctable, minor mortar bridging that can be cleared |
| **FAIL** | Missing vertical reinforcement, lap splice less than 25 in., unauthorized mechanical splice on rebar, wrong block type at bond beam, cells significantly clogged preventing grout flow, wall not plumb/level beyond tolerance |

---

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Confuses bond beam with lintel.** LLMs often treat "bond beam" and "lintel" as interchangeable. A bond beam is a continuous horizontal reinforced course that ties the wall together; a lintel spans over an opening. Different structural function, different reinforcement requirements.

2. **Ignores grout consolidation requirements.** AI rarely mentions that grout must be consolidated (rodded or vibrated) to fill around the rebar and eliminate voids. Unconsolidated grout is a common field failure.

3. **Underestimates Florida wind load requirements.** Generic AI responses use IRC minimum reinforcement. In Florida, especially in HVHZ or high-exposure areas, vertical and horizontal reinforcement spacing and size are often significantly increased. Always refer to the sealed plans.

4. **Treats any metal clamp on rebar as acceptable.** Wire rope clamps (U-bolt cable clamps) are NOT an ACI 318-approved mechanical splice. AI often fails to flag this because it doesn't recognize the visual difference between an approved coupler and a hardware-store clamp.

5. **Skips the cell cleanout inspection.** AI-generated checklists frequently omit verifying that grout cells are clean before the pour. Mortar droppings inside cells can block grout flow and create voids around reinforcement, compromising the structural connection.

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Vertical reinforcement missing from required cells | Fail → notify EOR immediately |
| Lap splice less than code minimum (25 in.) | Fail → bars must be extended or replaced per EOR direction |
| Wire rope clamps or unauthorized splices found | Fail → remove clamps, install proper lap splice |
| Bond beam block webs not knocked out | Hold → knockouts must be completed before grout |
| Significant mortar clogging in cells | Hold → clean cells before grout (may require partial disassembly) |
| Wall out of plumb beyond 1/4 in. per 4 ft | Hold → evaluate structural impact with EOR |
| Footing dowels not projecting into block cells | Fail → critical structural discontinuity → EOR redesign required |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Wide shot** — stem wall overview showing block courses and overall layout.
2. **Bond beam course** — close-up showing knockout block with horizontal channel.
3. **Horizontal bar** — bond beam reinforcement visible in the channel.
4. **Horizontal bar splice** — tape measure showing lap length.
5. **Vertical rebar continuity** — showing dowel from footing lapping with wall bar in cell.
6. **Vertical lap splice measurement** — tape measure showing 25 in. minimum.
7. **Cell interior** — flashlight/mirror shot showing clean cell ready for grout.
8. **Mortar droppings** — document any debris found (before and after cleanout).
9. **Section detail match** — photo showing the specific condition (A, B, or C) with plan detail for reference.
10. **Any deficiencies** — close-up with clear identification (especially unauthorized splices).
11. **Unauthorized splice example** — if wire rope clamp found, photograph with detail.
12. **Overview with context** — property, permit board, or address marker in frame.
