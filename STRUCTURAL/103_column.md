# Protocol 103: Column Inspection — Pre-Pour / Pre-Grout Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Columns
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 4 (Foundations) and Chapter 6 (Wall Construction)
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-pour (for cast-in-place concrete columns) or pre-grout (for CMU pier/columns) verification of reinforced columns in residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after reinforcement is placed but before concrete or grout placement**.

### In Scope
- Column cross-section dimensions (width, depth, height).
- Vertical reinforcement (bar size, count, spacing, position within column).
- Transverse ties and stirrups (size, spacing, hook orientation, lap splice zones).
- Lap splices on vertical bars (length, wire tie requirements).
- Clear cover for all reinforcement (formed concrete and masonry).
- CMU block type and cell conditions for grout placement.
- Column anchor bolts or mechanical connections.
- Formwork plumb, bracing, and cleanliness (cast-in-place columns).
- Column location verification (setbacks, grid alignment).

### Out of Scope
- Concrete mix design verification (batch tickets reviewed at pour).
- Post-pour or post-grout consolidation, honeycombing, or voids.
- Post-pour assessment of internal conditions (concealed after concrete/grout placement).
- Geotechnical bearing evaluation (see [Protocol 101](./101_footer_inspection.md)).
- Electrical grounding or embedment (see [Protocol 200](../ELECTRICAL/200_footer_bond_inspection.md)).
- CMU cell grout placement procedures (see [Protocol 121](./121_fill_cells.md)).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the column is ready for concrete placement (cast-in-place) or grout placement (CMU) by verifying:
- **PASS:** All dimensions, reinforcement, spacing, and position meet plans and FBC-R/ACI 318.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before placement authorization.
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring redesign or re-forming/reconstruction.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Do not** enter interior of tall columns or formwork without safety equipment and supervision.
- **Do not** disturb or reposition placed reinforcement or move bars to "test" placement or spacing.
- **Do not** cut or remove ties to verify lap splice length — use visual measurement only.
- **Do not** apply loads to formwork or bracing to test stability.
- **Stop condition:** If formwork shows active movement, collapse risk, or instability — STOP and notify the contractor and structural engineer before proceeding. Do not climb inside columns.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Column Dimensions

1. Measure column **width** and **depth** (cross-sectional dimensions) at a minimum of 3 elevations.
   - Compare to architectural/structural plans.
   - Typical residential columns: 8 in. × 8 in. to 12 in. × 12 in. (verify per plans).
   - For CMU columns: measure block dimensions and verify block type (8-in., 10-in., 12-in. nominal).
2. Measure column **height** from base to top.
   - Verify against plans (clear height or floor-to-floor dimension).
3. Check that formwork (if cast-in-place) is **plumb** in both directions using a level or plumb bob.
   - Maximum acceptable plumb deviation per ACI 301: 1/8 in. in 10 ft (or 1% height, whichever is less).

### Step 2: Verify Column Location

1. Measure column location relative to building grid or property lines per plans.
   - Check setbacks, distances to walls, and grid alignment.
2. Verify vertical alignment between column base and top.
   - Plumb tolerance per ACI 301: 1/8 in. in 10 ft.
3. Confirm column does not conflict with utilities, existing structure, or architectural features.

### Step 3: Verify Vertical Reinforcement (Main Bars)

1. Count the number of vertical bars.
   - Typical residential: 4 bars for #5 size, or 2 bars for #6–#8 size (verify per plans).
2. Verify **bar size** using deformation pattern or bar markings.
   - #3 = 3/8 in. diameter, #4 = 1/2 in., #5 = 5/8 in., #6 = 3/4 in.
3. Verify bar **position and spacing** within the column cross-section.
   - Bars must be uniformly spaced and positioned to provide adequate clear cover (see Step 6).
   - Corner bars must be present (never relying on ties to carry load at corners).
4. Check **lap splices** (if present at construction joints or growth locations):
   - Minimum lap length for #5 bars: typically 25 in. (verify per ACI 318-19, Table 25.5.2, for applicable concrete strength).
   - For #6 bars: typically 30 in. minimum.
   - Lap splices must be tied with wire ties — not loose.
   - Stagger splices in the column (not all four bars splicing at the same elevation).
5. Verify bars are **continuous from base to top** or properly spliced if growth is required.

### Step 4: Verify Transverse Reinforcement (Ties and Stirrups)

1. Verify **tie size** (typically #3 bars for columns, per plans).
   - Tie size must be at least 1/4 of the vertical bar diameter per ACI 318.
2. Measure **spacing** of ties along the column height.
   - Standard spacing: 12 in. O.C. in non-critical zones (verify per plans).
   - Spacing must not exceed the least column dimension or 12 in. (ACI 318-19, 10.7.6.2.3).
3. Verify **hook orientation** on ties:
   - ACI 318 requires **135-degree hooks** at the end of ties around the vertical bars.
   - Hooks must engage the vertical bars and be pulled tight (not bent loosely).
   - Common error: 90-degree corner bends are **NOT compliant** — must be 135-degree hooks.
4. Check tie **hook length**:
   - Minimum: 6 times the tie bar diameter (typically 2.25 in. for #3 tie).
   - Verify hooks extend sufficiently into the column core.

### Step 5: Verify Tie Spacing in Lap Splice Zones

1. Identify all **vertical bar lap splice locations** in the column.
2. Measure spacing of ties in the lap splice zone:
   - ACI 318-19 requires closer tie spacing in lap zones: spacing = **1/4 of the vertical bar diameter** (not to exceed 6 in. for #5 bars = 1.5 in. spacing approximately).
   - Example: #5 vertical bars (5/8 in. diameter) require ties at maximum 6 in. spacing, but in lap zones must reduce to ~1.5 in. spacing.
   - This reduced spacing must extend from the start of the splice to 12 in. beyond the splice end.
3. Verify this increased confinement is shown on the plans and properly installed.

### Step 6: Verify Clear Cover

1. Measure **clear cover** from the exposed surface to the nearest reinforcement.
   - For **formed concrete columns** (cast-in-place): minimum 1.5 in. (ACI 318-19, 20.6.1.2 for general structural members exposed to weather).
   - For **CMU masonry columns**: minimum 1.5 in. from the inner face of the block (TMS 402).
2. Verify that reinforcement is supported at the correct depth using chairs, spacers, or hangers.
   - Rebar must NOT rest on the ground or formwork base directly.
   - Spacer blocks or rebar chairs must be corrosion-resistant (plastic coated or non-corrosive material for outdoor exposure).

### Step 7: Verify CMU Block Conditions (For Masonry Columns)

1. Confirm **block type and nominal size** match plans (8-in., 10-in., or 12-in.).
2. Verify **cell dimensions** are adequate for rebar placement:
   - Rebar and ties must fit within the block cells with adequate cover.
   - No interference with block webs.
3. Check that **cells are clean** and free of mortar droppings, debris, or obstructions:
   - Debris blocks grout flow and compromises column strength.
4. Verify **head joints** (vertical mortar) are filled between blocks (no voids at block interface).

### Step 8: Verify Anchor Bolts and Connection Hardware

1. If the column supports a beam or carries mechanical loads:
   - Verify **anchor bolt size, spacing, and embedment length** per plans.
   - Anchor bolts must be properly positioned in the formwork or CMU cells.
   - Confirm bolts are tied or held in position — will not shift during pour/grout.
2. Check for any **mechanical connection hardware** (steel brackets, clips, etc.) and verify positioning.
3. Confirm embedment depth and orientation match the structural connection detail.

### Step 9: Verify Formwork Condition (Cast-in-Place Only)

1. Check formwork **interior surface** is clean and free of debris, loose mortar, or obstructions.
2. Verify **bracing** is in place and adequate to resist concrete pressure (typical wall pressure ~150 psf for first meter).
3. Check that formwork does not have **large gaps, splits, or deformation**:
   - Minor surface imperfections are acceptable.
   - Gaps or deformation that would create large voids or honeycombing are a concern.
4. Confirm **tie-backs and spreaders** are properly installed and will not create voids in the concrete.

### Step 10: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the full height of the column and visually inspect all faces.
2. Note the overall appearance, any obvious deficiencies, or red flags.
3. Photograph:
   - Overall column exterior and perimeter (wide shot showing full height).
   - Vertical rebar count and spacing (close-up with scale).
   - Tie spacing and hook detail (close-up showing 135-degree bend).
   - Lap splice location with measurement.
   - Clear cover verification (scale placed against rebar).
   - Formwork or CMU condition.
   - Column location and grid alignment.
   - Any deficiencies or concerns with annotations.
4. Record all measurements and observations on the field inspection form.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | Column dimensions match plans, vertical bar count/size/position correct, tie size/spacing and 135-degree hooks compliant, lap splices adequate and staggered, clear cover verified, formwork plumb or CMU cells clean, location matches plans |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable on-site before pour (e.g., one tie spacing slightly off, minor formwork gap, minor position adjustment with proper bracing) |
| **FAIL** | Wrong bar size or count, tie spacing grossly non-compliant, 90-degree hooks instead of 135-degree (non-compliant), lap splice length inadequate, clear cover less than minimum, formwork significantly out of plumb (>1/8 in. in 10 ft), CMU cells blocked with debris |

---

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Confuses tie hook requirements.** ACI 318 requires **135-degree hooks** on column ties to properly confine the core and engage vertical bars. LLMs frequently cite 90-degree corner bends as acceptable. A 90-degree bend is NOT a compliant hook — this is a critical deficiency.

2. **Gets tie spacing wrong in lap splice zones.** AI often ignores the requirement for closer tie spacing in the vicinity of vertical bar lap splices. ACI 318 requires spacing of approximately 1/4 of the vertical bar diameter in the lap zone. Standard 12-in. spacing throughout the column is non-compliant in lap regions and will be flagged by any competent inspector.

3. **Applies beam tie spacing to columns.** Beam stirrup spacing (16–20 in.) is not the same as column tie spacing. Columns, especially in residential high-rise or load-heavy conditions, require tighter confinement. Generic LLM responses that cite beam guidance are incomplete.

4. **Ignores minimum column dimension requirements.** FBC-R and ACI 318 specify minimum column dimensions based on load and unbraced length. AI may cite arbitrary column sizes without checking code minimums, leading to approval of undersized columns.

5. **Treats CMU pier columns and cast-in-place columns as identical.** These follow different code paths: CMU columns are governed by TMS 402, while cast-in-place columns follow ACI 318. Grout cell conditions, block orientation, and cover requirements differ. Conflating the two is a major inspection oversight.

- [ ] Lap splice length and location (if applicable).
- [ ] Clear cover measurements (minimum 3 points).

- [ ] Formwork plumb verification (cast-in-place) or CMU cell condition notes (masonry).
- [ ] Anchor bolt size, spacing, and embedment (if applicable).

- [ ] Final disposition: PASS / HOLD / FAIL with PE signature.

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Vertical bar count/size does not match sealed plans | Hold pour/grout → notify EOR (Engineer of Record) for resolution |
| Tie spacing exceeds 12 in. O.C. in non-lap zones (or plan requirement) | Hold pour/grout → contractor must install additional ties or brace removal |
| Tie hooks are 90-degree (not 135-degree) | Fail → ties must be removed and replaced with compliant hooks per ACI 318 |
| Lap splice length is less than code minimum | Fail → require mechanical coupler or compliant lap re-installation |
| Clear cover is less than 1.5 in. | Hold → may proceed with approved cover mitigation or rebar repositioning |
| CMU cells blocked with mortar or debris | Hold grout → cells must be cleaned before grout placement |
| Formwork significantly out of plumb (>1/8 in. in 10 ft) | Hold pour → formwork must be braced and re-checked for plumb |
| Column location off grid by >1 in. | Hold → verify against structural plans; may require shim or EOR approval |
| Anchor bolts missing or misaligned | Fail → bolts must be correctly positioned before pour/grout |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Column overview** — full height and perimeter view showing overall column location and context.
2. **Grid location** — column position relative to building grid or architectural reference.
3. **Vertical reinforcement** — close-up of top or middle section showing bar count, deformation pattern, and spacing.
4. **Vertical bar detail** — individual bar showing size markings (deformation pattern) with scale reference.
5. **Tie spacing (standard zone)** — tape measure showing spacing between consecutive ties in non-lap area.
6. **Tie hook detail** — close-up of tie hook showing 135-degree angle and engagement with vertical bars.
7. **Lap splice location** — if present, tape measure showing splice length alongside the vertical bars.
8. **Tie spacing (lap zone)** — if applicable, tape measure showing reduced spacing in lap splice region.
9. **Clear cover** — scale placed between formwork/block face and nearest rebar.
10. **Formwork plumb check** — level or plumb bob showing vertical alignment of formwork.
11. **CMU cell condition** — close-up of clean cells and block type (for masonry columns).
12. **Anchor bolts or connections** — close-up showing position, size, and embedment (if applicable).
