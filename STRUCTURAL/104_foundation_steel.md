# Protocol 104: Foundation Steel — Complete System Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Foundation
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 4 (Foundations)
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-pour verification of the complete foundation reinforcing steel package for single-family residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after all rebar placement but before concrete placement**, and addresses the overall integration of foundation elements including footings, grade beams, pile caps (if applicable), tie beams, and interconnecting reinforcement.

### In Scope
- Bar size and grade matching the structural steel schedule.
- Bar count and placement at each foundation element (footings, grade beams, pile caps, tie beams).
- Lap splice lengths, locations, and stagger per ACI 318.
- Clear cover for reinforcement based on exposure condition.
- Rebar support and positioning (chairs, bolsters, spacers).
- Hooks and standard bends per ACI 318.
- Continuity of reinforcement at corners, intersections, and element transitions.
- Bar surface condition (rust, contamination, bond integrity).
- Authorized mechanical and welded splice compliance.

### Out of Scope
- Post-pour honeycombing or consolidation checks.
- Concrete compressive strength testing (batch tickets at pour).
- Geotechnical bearing capacity (see geotechnical report).
- Footer bond / Ufer ground inspection (see [Protocol 200](../ELECTRICAL/200_footer_bond_inspection.md)).
- Specific foundation element details covered separately (see [Protocol 101](./101_footer_inspection.md) for footings, [Protocol 103](./103_column.md) for columns, [Protocol 105](./105_foundation_walls_stem_walls.md) for stem walls).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the complete foundation steel system is ready for concrete placement by verifying:
- **PASS:** All steel elements are correctly sized, placed, and connected per structural plans and FBC-R.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before pour authorization.
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring corrective action or design review.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Do not** disturb, move, or reposition placed reinforcement during inspection.
- **Do not** perform destructive testing (bending, striking, cutting) on reinforcement.
- **Do not** climb on or apply load to rebar in a way that could shift placement.
- **Trench entry:** Do not enter excavations deeper than 4 ft without shoring or sloping per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P.
- **Stop condition:** If reinforcement shows evidence of damage, kinking, or deformation from handling — STOP and notify the contractor and EOR (Engineer of Record) before proceeding.

- [ ] All rebar placement is complete and supported (chairs, bolsters, spacers visible).

- [ ] No utilities or embedded items are interfering with reinforcement placement.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Steel Schedule Against Placed Rebar

1. Obtain the structural steel schedule from the sealed plans.
   - Steel schedule lists: bar size, location (footer, grade beam, pile cap, tie beam, dowels), bar grade, and quantity per element.
2. Systematically verify each element type:
   - **Footings:** Longitudinal bar count and size per [Protocol 101](./101_footer_inspection.md).
   - **Columns:** Longitudinal and confinement steel per [Protocol 103](./103_column.md).
   - **Grade beams:** Top and bottom steel, side cover, overall depth.
   - **Pile caps (if applicable):** Top and bottom steel per cap design, edge conditions.
   - **Tie beams:** Connection bars between columns or grade beams.
3. Compare actual bar sizes to the schedule using deformation pattern or bar markings.
   - Do not rely on visual appearance alone — read the mill marking on the bar.
   - #4 = 1/2 in. diameter, #5 = 5/8 in. diameter, #6 = 3/4 in. diameter.

### Step 2: Verify Bar Count and Placement at Each Element

1. **Footings:**
   - Count continuous longitudinal bars at multiple locations along the footing run.
   - Verify count matches the steel schedule (e.g., "2 ea. #5 continuous").
   - Check that no bars are missing or cut short in the middle of a span.
2. **Grade beams:**
   - Count top steel and bottom steel separately.
   - Verify spacing matches plans (e.g., "3 ea. #5 bottom, equally spaced").
3. **Pile caps:**
   - Count bars in both directions (if grid reinforcement).
   - Verify corner and edge bars are present and positioned.
4. **Tie beams:**
   - Verify longitudinal bars connecting foundation elements.
   - Check that ties span the full length between supports.
5. **Dowels for future stem wall connection:**
   - Verify projection height above top of footing.
   - Count spacing (e.g., "48 in. O.C." or as specified).

### Step 3: Verify Lap Splice Lengths and Locations

1. Identify all lap splices in the foundation steel.
   - Lap splices are where two bars overlap and are tied together.
2. Measure splice length:
   - Minimum lap for #5 bars: typically 25 in. (consult plans for exact requirement per concrete strength and bar grade).
   - Minimum lap for #6 bars: typically 30 in.
   - Minimum lap for #4 bars: typically 20 in.
   - Verify per ACI 318-19, Chapter 25 for tension vs. compression splices.
3. Check splice **stagger**:
   - If two or more bars splice in the same element, splices must be staggered.
   - **Red flag:** Both bars splicing at the same location reduces connection efficiency.
4. Verify splices are **tied with wire ties**:
   - Ties should be taut and evenly spaced (typically every 12 in. along the splice).
   - Splices must not rely on friction or weight alone.
5. Check that splices are **not located at high-stress zones** (corners, intersections) unless specifically detailed.

### Step 4: Verify Clear Cover

1. Measure clear cover from the exposed surface to the nearest bar:
   - **Cast-against-earth (footings, grade beams):** 3 in. minimum (ACI 318-19, 20.6.1.3).
   - **Formed foundation members (columns, walls, tie beams) not exposed to earth:** 2 in. minimum.
   - **Formed members with #5 bars and smaller:** 1.5 in. minimum (if plans specify).
2. Use a depth gauge or folding ruler to measure from the forming edge or ground surface.
3. **Red flag:** If clear cover is less than required, bars may become exposed after concrete placement.
4. Verify that all reinforcement is supported on chairs, bolsters, or spacers:
   - Bottom steel must not rest directly on earth or forms.
   - Support devices should maintain consistent spacing along the bar run.

### Step 5: Verify Hooks and Bends

1. Check all standard hooks (typically at dowel bottoms or grade beam ends):
   - Standard hook for #5 bar: **10 in. minimum** (measured along the bar axis).
   - Standard hook for #6 bar: **12 in. minimum**.
   - Verify hook orientation engages the longitudinal reinforcement.
2. Measure bend radii for non-standard bends:
   - Bend radius must accommodate bar size to avoid cracking or fracture.
   - Verify against ACI 318 bend diameter requirements (typically 4–6× bar diameter).
3. Check that no bar is kinked or bent unintentionally:
   - Kinks from handling reduce bar strength and bond.

### Step 6: Verify Continuity and Interconnection at Corners and Intersections

1. **Corners:**
   - Verify that longitudinal bars turn corners with proper hooks or 90-degree bends.
   - Check that no bars are cut short at corners.
2. **Intersections:**
   - Where grade beams meet footings, verify that connection bars are present (dowels or lap splices).
   - Where tie beams connect columns, verify that the connection steel matches the design detail.
3. **Element transitions:**
   - Verify continuity where foundation elements change (e.g., footing to stem wall).
   - Check that dowels extend the required lap splice length above the footing.

### Step 7: Verify Bar Surface Condition

1. **Visual inspection for rust:**
   - Light surface rust (mill scale) is normal and acceptable — it actually improves bond.
   - **Red flag:** Loose rust scale that can be easily wiped off, flaking rust, or deep pitting indicates corrosion.
   - Bar should not have excessive corrosion that reduces its cross-sectional area.
2. **Check for contamination:**
   - Bars must be free of mud, dirt, grease, oil, paint, or coatings that would impair bond.
   - Clean off loose soil or debris before pouring.
3. **Verify bar straightness:**
   - Bars bent during shipping or handling may not develop proper bond.
   - Check that bars lie flat and straight in their final position.

### Step 8: Verify Bar Grade and Markings

1. Inspect bar markings on the bar surface:
   - Grade 60 bars are marked with "60" (standard for residential).
   - Grade 75 or Grade 80 bars are marked accordingly (less common for residential).
2. If ASTM A706 (low-alloy) bars are specified, verify markings include "706".
3. **Red flag:** Unmarked bars or bars marked with unfamiliar grade — do not accept without EOR approval.

### Step 9: Verify Authorized Splice Methods

1. Identify all splice locations and verify the splice method:
   - **Lap splices** (overlapped bars tied with wire) — standard method, requires proper lap length.
   - **Mechanical couplers** (registered in ACI 318) — must verify model and installation per ACI 318, 25.4.
   - **Welded splices** (per AWS D1.4) — must verify welder certification and weld inspection requirements.
2. **Red flag:** Wire rope clamps (U-bolt or cable clamps) are **NOT an approved splice method** per ACI 318.
   - If found, FAIL the inspection and require removal.
3. Check that no unauthorized methods (epoxied bars, pressure-fit splices, etc.) are present.

### Step 10: Verify Rebar Support and Spacing

1. Check that all reinforcement is supported:
   - Chairs and bolsters for longitudinal bars (typically 4–6 in. up from forms/ground).
   - Spacers maintaining clear distance between bars.
2. Verify spacing between bars:
   - Minimum clear spacing typically 1.5× bar diameter (verify per plans).
   - Bars must not be squeezed together or doubled up to occupy less space.
3. Check that support devices are stable and will not shift during concrete placement.

### Step 11: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire foundation perimeter and systematically check each element (footing runs, grade beams, pile caps, columns, tie beams).
2. Cross-reference the inspection against the steel schedule to confirm all bars are accounted for.
3. Photograph:
   - Overall foundation layout (wide shot showing multiple elements).
   - Steel schedule (photo of the sealed plan page for reference).
   - Each foundation element type with bar count visible.
   - Lap splice locations with tape measure showing length.
   - Vertical dowels with spacing measurement.
   - Hooks and bends at connections.
   - Rebar support (chairs, spacers).
   - Bar surface condition (if any rust or contamination).
   - Any unauthorized splices or deficiencies.
4. Record all measurements and bar identifications on the field inspection form.
5. Note any element that deviates from the plans or steel schedule.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All bar sizes, counts, and placements match the steel schedule; splice lengths and stagger correct; cover adequate; hooks and bends proper; bar surface clean; all interconnections verified; no unauthorized splice methods. |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable on-site without design change (e.g., one bar out of position but no safety impact, missing support chair, single tie-down needed). Contractor must make correction before pour. |
| **FAIL** | Bar size mismatch, missing bars, splice length inadequate, unauthorized splice method, clear cover significantly deficient, bar damaged or kinked, or bars not interconnected per design. Requires EOR review and corrective action before pour. |

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Applies a single cover value to all foundation conditions.** LLMs frequently cite 1.5 in. or 2 in. cover universally. Clear cover varies: 3 in. for cast-against-earth (footings, grade beams on ground), 2 in. for formed members, 1.5 in. for formed members with #5 bars and smaller. Ignoring this difference is a critical error.

2. **Ignores bar condition and accepts any rebar.** AI often states that any rust is unacceptable. Light surface rust (mill scale) is normal and acceptable — it actually improves bond. Loose rust scale and deep corrosion are red flags. Bar surface must be evaluated for bond impact, not cosmetic appearance alone.

3. **Treats all splices as identical.** AI conflates compression and tension splice requirements. ACI 318 specifies different lap lengths for tension vs. compression splices. Tension splices (bottom steel in beams) typically require longer lap than compression splices. The inspection must distinguish the type.

4. **Ignores Florida wind and exposure requirements.** Generic AI responses default to IRC minimums. FBC residential construction in high-velocity hurricane zones often requires additional reinforcement beyond IRC standards. The steel schedule reflects these requirements; the inspection verifies compliance.

5. **Misidentifies bar sizes from deformation patterns.** AI sometimes guesses bar size from appearance. Bar size must be verified by reading the mill marking on the bar — this is the only reliable method. Deformation patterns are similar across sizes and easy to misread.

- [ ] Name and PE license number of inspecting engineer.
- [ ] Plan sheet reference (foundation plan and steel schedule revision and date).
- [ ] Steel schedule verification (bar size, count, location for each element type).
- [ ] Lap splice length measurements and locations (minimum 3 splices per element type).
- [ ] Clear cover measurements at multiple locations.
- [ ] Vertical dowel projection height and spacing measurements.
- [ ] Rebar support verification (chair type, spacing, condition).
- [ ] Bar surface condition notes (rust, contamination, straightness).
- [ ] Splice method verification (lap vs. mechanical vs. welded).
- [ ] Photo set with scale reference (minimum 12 photos per inspection: overall layout, each element type, splices, hooks, support, deficiencies).
- [ ] Deficiency list (if any) with required corrective actions.
- [ ] Cross-check against steel schedule (all bars accounted for or discrepancies noted).
- [ ] Final disposition: PASS / HOLD / FAIL with PE signature.
- [ ] Limitations statement: "This inspection is limited to visual observation of accessible conditions at the time of inspection. Concealed conditions after concrete placement are excluded. Compliance is verified against the sealed structural plans at the time of inspection."

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Bar size or count does not match steel schedule | Hold pour → notify EOR for resolution |
| Lap splice length is inadequate | Fail → require corrective splice (lengthen, add coupler, or re-pull bar) |
| Unauthorized splice method (wire rope clamps, etc.) found | Fail → require removal and replacement with approved method |
| Clear cover significantly deficient (less than schedule) | Hold pour → notify EOR for design review or bar repositioning |
| Bar damaged, kinked, or deformed | Hold pour → notify EOR for strength evaluation or bar replacement |
| Missing or cut short bars | Fail → EOR must authorize modification or bars must be installed |
| Bars not tied or secured (loose placement) | Hold pour → contractor must secure all steel before pour |
| Unmarked bars or unverified bar grade | Hold pour → require mill certification or bar replacement |
| Interconnection missing at element transitions | Fail → add required dowels or lap splices per plans |
| Heavy corrosion or deep pitting on rebar | Hold pour → contractor must clean or replace affected bars |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Overall foundation layout** — wide shot showing multiple elements (footings, grade beams, columns) from ground level.
2. **Steel schedule reference** — photo of the sealed plans showing the bar size, count, and location table.
3. **Footing longitudinal steel** — close-up showing bar count, size (deformation pattern visible), and continuity along the run.
4. **Grade beam steel** — close-up showing top and bottom bars, count, and spacing.
5. **Lap splice detail** — tape measure showing splice length alongside the overlapped bars.
6. **Vertical dowels** — close-up showing hook at bottom, projection height above footing, and spacing between consecutive dowels.
7. **Rebar support** — close-up showing chair, bolster, or spacer maintaining clear cover and bar spacing.
8. **Bar surface condition** — close-up of bar showing finish (mill scale acceptable, rust flaking or contamination if present).
9. **Hooks and bends** — detail shot of standard hook or 90-degree bend at connections.
10. **Pile cap or tie beam connection** — showing how reinforcement interconnects between elements.
11. **Element intersection** — corner or junction detail showing continuity of steel.
12. **Any deficiencies** — close-up with clear identification of the issue (missing bar, loose splice, unauthorized clamp, etc.).
13. **Project context** — property address, permit board, or site marker in frame.
