# Protocol 105: Foundation Walls / Stem Walls — Pre-Grout Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Foundation
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 4 (Foundations) and Chapter 6 (Wall Construction)
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-grout verification of CMU (concrete masonry unit) stem walls for single-family residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after masonry blocks are laid and vertical/horizontal reinforcement is placed, but before grouting**. The stem wall is the loadbearing masonry wall between the footer and the slab that forms the perimeter foundation.

### In Scope
- Block type, size, and layout (8×8×16 CMU, loadbearing per ASTM C90).
- Block coursing and vertical alignment.
- Mortar joint consistency, thickness, and bedding.
- Vertical reinforcement (bar size, spacing, lap splices with footer dowels, centering in cells, cover).
- Horizontal reinforcement (bond beam courses, knockout blocks, bar size, continuity, splices).
- Shearwall reinforcement (if applicable per wind/seismic schedule).
- Wall level and plumb.
- Anchor bolt placement for sill plate (if at this stage).
- Header blocks and opening details.
- Cell cleanliness and readiness for grout (no mortar bridging or obstructions).

### Out of Scope
- Mortar batch and curing (reviewed at time of laying).
- Grout placement and consolidation (see [Protocol 121](./121_fill_cells.md)).
- Post-grout compressive testing or core sampling.
- Header lintel design verification (cross-reference structural drawings).
- Detailed shearwall computation (reference design engineer's shearwall schedule).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the CMU stem wall is ready for grouting by verifying:
- **PASS:** All block, mortar, reinforcement, and cell conditions meet plans and FBC-R.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before grouting (e.g., clean cells, repoint joints).
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies (wrong block type, misaligned bars, missing reinforcement) requiring correction or redesign.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Working height:** Use fall protection per OSHA if wall height exceeds 4 ft. Inspect from ladder or ground where possible.
- **Do not** remove, reposition, or "test" placed reinforcement bars or blocks.
- **Do not** strike mortar joints or probe block cells with force that could dislodge or crack units.
- **Do not** use destructive methods to measure bar cover (no coring, no breaking blocks).
- **Do not** apply pressure or probe cells with tools larger than 3/8 in. diameter — this can cause bridging in cells intended for grout.
- **Stop condition:** If wall shows active cracking, shifting, or instability during observation — STOP and notify the contractor and structural engineer before proceeding.
- **Mortar observation limit:** Visual inspection only; do not remove mortar samples for strength testing (mortar testing is a separate geotechnical or materials sampling activity, not part of this field inspection).

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Block Type, Size, and Layout

1. Confirm CMU block type:
   - Loadbearing concrete masonry units (CMU) per ASTM C90.
   - Typical residential Florida stem walls: **8×8×16 nominal** (actual: 7.625 in. × 7.625 in. × 15.625 in.).
   - Verify block meets minimum f'm (compressive strength) per engineer's specification (typically 1,900–2,000 psi for residential).
2. Confirm block is **solid or fully grouted cells** (not hollow blocks used in non-loadbearing partitions).
3. Verify block layout matches plans:
   - Stretcher courses (long face out) for typical wall construction.
   - No unauthorized block substitutions or different CMU types in same wall.
4. Check for **cracked, broken, or damaged blocks**:
   - Small hairline cracks (< 1/32 in.) in interior cells may be acceptable if not at reinforcement location.
   - Larger cracks or spalls: consult structural engineer for acceptability.

### Step 2: Verify Vertical Course Count and Wall Height

1. Count courses from top of footer to top of wall:
   - Verify count matches plan elevation.
   - Typical residential: 4–6 courses depending on design (footer to slab height).
2. Measure total wall height:
   - From top of footer to top of wall (before slab).
   - Should match plan dimension ± 1/4 in. over total height (reasonable tolerance for hand-laid masonry).
3. Check course-to-course consistency:
   - Each course should be approximately 8 in. nominal (with 3/8 in. mortar joint).
   - Variation in course thickness indicates mortar joint thickness variation; flag for review.

### Step 3: Verify Mortar Joint Consistency and Quality

1. **Joint thickness:**
   - Target nominal: **3/8 in.** per ASTM C270 and NCMA guidelines.
   - Acceptable range: 5/16 in. to 7/16 in.
   - Measure a minimum of 10 joints (horizontal and vertical) at different elevations.
2. **Mortar type:**
   - Verify Type N or Type S mortar per plans and ASTM C270 (Type N is typical for residential CMU).
3. **Bedding quality:**
   - **Full bedding:** Mortar on all horizontal surfaces (top and bottom of blocks) across entire block width.
   - **Back beveling:** Slight mortar recess behind outer shell acceptable, but no voids at interior block faces.
   - **Vertical joints:** Filled completely between block end faces (no gaps or hollow spaces).
4. **Red flag:** Consistently thin joints (< 1/4 in.), gaps, or hollow mortar beds indicate poor workmanship and risk of water infiltration and reduced compressive capacity.

### Step 4: Verify Vertical Reinforcement (Bar Size, Spacing, Lap, Cover)

1. **Bar identification:**
   - Confirm vertical bar size matches plans (typically **#5 bars** for residential stem walls).
   - Read bar markings: #5 = 5/8 in. diameter, #4 = 1/2 in. diameter.
   - Use rebar gauge if markings are unclear.

2. **Spacing:**
   - Verify spacing matches engineer's schedule (typically **48 in. O.C., 32 in. O.C., or 24 in. O.C.** depending on design).
   - Measure distances between consecutive vertical bars at a minimum of 2 locations per wall run.
   - Tolerance: ±2 in. is reasonable; beyond that is a deficiency.

3. **Lap splice with footer dowels:**
   - **Critical:** Each vertical stem wall bar must lap with a dowel projecting from the footer below.
   - Lap splice length for #5 bars in grouted masonry: **minimum 25 in.** (per ACI 318 and TMS 402/602).
   - Measure lap length: distance from bottom of vertical bar to top of dowel below.
     - If dowel projects 25 in. above top of footer, vertical bar starts at that level and extends upward, providing the lap.
   - Verify **both bars are tied together** with wire ties at top and bottom of lap zone (minimum 2 ties per lap).
   - **Both bars must be in the same cell** — no bar splices occurring outside the cell or in adjacent cells.

4. **Bar centering and cover:**
   - Each vertical bar must be **centered in its cell** for minimum 1.5 in. clear cover on all sides per TMS 402/602.
   - Verify centering:
     - Measure distance from bar center to nearest interior block wall face — should be ≥ 1.5 in.
     - Use a rebar center gauge or caliper to verify if bar appears off-center.
   - **Red flag:** Bar touching or within 1 in. of block face or mortar joint — inadequate cover.

5. **Bar continuity:**
   - Verify no horizontal offsets or bends in vertical bars (bars must remain plumb).
   - Confirm bars are properly supported (tied to horizontal reinforcement or support ties) to prevent shifting during grout.

### Step 5: Verify Horizontal Reinforcement and Bond Beams

**Critical distinction:** A **bond beam** is a reinforced course that ties the wall together horizontally; a **lintel** spans over an opening. These have different structural functions and different reinforcement requirements. This inspection covers bond beams only.

1. **Bond beam location:**
   - Identify bond beam courses per plans (typically at top of wall, but may be required at intermediate locations for tall walls).
   - Verify bond beam course is present at each required elevation.

2. **Bond beam block:**
   - Confirm **knockout block** (also called bond beam block or lintel block) is used, not standard CMU.
   - Knockout blocks have **webs removed or cut out** to create a continuous channel for horizontal reinforcement.
   - **Red flag:** Standard solid CMU installed in place of knockout block prevents continuous horizontal bar; this is a critical deficiency.

3. **Horizontal bar size and continuity:**
   - Confirm horizontal bar size matches plans (typically **#5 or #4** for residential bond beams).
   - Verify bar runs **continuously** through the bond beam course (no gaps, no splice in middle of wall run).
   - Measure length of continuous bar at minimum 2 locations.

4. **Horizontal bar splices:**
   - If splices are required (wall longer than one bar length):
     - Minimum lap splice: **25 in. for #5 bars**, 20 in. for #4 bars (per ACI 318).
     - Splices must occur at corners or designated locations per plans, not arbitrary mid-wall.
     - Both bars in splice must be **in the same bond beam cell/channel**.
     - Verify splice is **tied** with wire ties (minimum 2 ties).

5. **Bond beam reinforcement spacing (shearwall requirement):**
   - If shearwall reinforcement is required (per wind/seismic design):
     - Verify **horizontal bar spacing** in shearwall courses (may be closer than non-shearwall bond beams).
     - Reference shearwall schedule on plans (e.g., "#5 @ 16 in. O.C." for shearwall sections).
     - Common horizontal shearwall spacing in Florida high-wind zones: **16 in., 12 in., or 8 in. O.C.** (tighter spacing = greater shear capacity).
   - Measure spacing at minimum 2 locations in shearwall sections.

6. **Bond beam cap block or cover:**
   - Verify cell openings above bond beam are covered by a solid cap block or cover block to allow proper grouting and prevent debris/water intrusion.
   - If open channel remains exposed, grout cannot be poured properly.

### Step 6: Verify Wall Level and Plumb

1. **Horizontal level (top of wall):**
   - Check top edge of wall for level across full length.
   - Use a 4–6 ft level at minimum of 3 locations per wall run.
   - Tolerance: ±1/4 in. over 10 ft is reasonable for masonry construction (consult engineer if deviation exceeds this).

2. **Vertical plumb:**
   - Check wall face for plumb (vertical alignment).
   - Use level at minimum of 3 locations per wall run (left side, center, right side).
   - Use plumb bob or laser level for accuracy over wall height.
   - Tolerance: ±1/4 in. over 10 ft of height.
   - **Red flag:** Wall leaning, bulging, or offset > 1/2 in. — STOP and notify engineer.

3. **Block face alignment:**
   - Use straightedge against block face to check for waviness or misalignment.
   - Minor undulation due to hand-laid blocks is acceptable; gross misalignment indicates poor workmanship.

### Step 7: Verify Anchor Bolt Placement (Sill Plate Connection)

1. **Anchor bolt location:**
   - If anchor bolts for sill plate are installed at this stage (before grout):
     - Verify location matches plans (typically 4–6 ft O.C., or as specified).
     - Bolts should be embedded in grouted cells or bond beam per plans.
2. **Bolt alignment:**
   - Verify bolts are **vertical and plumb** (can be checked with plumb bob or level).
   - Spacing tolerance: ±2 in. from plan location.
3. **Bolt depth:**
   - Confirm bolt is embedded sufficiently (typically 7–10 in. minimum into concrete/grout) per ACI 318.
   - Note: Detailed anchor bolt verification occurs at slab inspection (Protocol 106); this step is precursory.

### Step 8: Verify Header Blocks and Opening Details

1. **Header block placement:**
   - Verify header blocks (solid masonry units) are installed above any openings (windows, doors, vents) in the stem wall.
   - Header blocks must span the opening or support a lintel (separate reinforced element above opening).
2. **Lintel vs. bond beam distinction:**
   - **Lintel:** Reinforced element (masonry or reinforced concrete) spanning over an opening — carries load from wall above the opening.
   - **Bond beam:** Reinforced course tying wall together horizontally — does not span concentrated opening loads.
   - Verify that lintels (if present) have reinforcement per structural engineer's detail (different from bond beam spacing/sizing).
   - Cross-check opening schedule on structural plans.

### Step 9: Verify Cleanout Openings (Tall Walls)

1. **Cleanout opening requirement:**
   - Per TMS 602, walls taller than 4 courses (approximately 32 in. nominal) typically require **cleanout openings** at the bottom of each column of cells.
   - Cleanout openings allow access to remove mortar droppings and debris from cell cavities before grouting.
2. **Location and size:**
   - Cleanouts are usually located in the bottom course or second course.
   - Typical size: 2–3 in. diameter, or a removable block.
3. **Verification:**
   - Confirm cleanout openings are present and accessible.
   - Verify they are **unobstructed and ready to be sealed before grouting**.
   - Red flag: Missing cleanout openings in tall walls indicates inadequate design or incomplete construction.

### Step 10: Verify Cell Cleanliness and Grout Readiness

1. **Visual inspection of cells:**
   - Look into each cell that will receive vertical reinforcement or will be grouted.
   - Shine light into cells to identify debris, mortar droppings, or obstructions.
   - **Red flag conditions:**
     - Mortar "bridging" (mortar forming a shelf or ledge inside the cell that prevents grout flow).
     - Loose mortar fragments or concrete debris.
     - Standing water or moisture.
     - Visible gaps or voids at block interfaces.

2. **Mortar bridging check:**
   - Mortar droppings accumulate at interior block faces during laying.
   - Bridging occurs when mortar hardens and creates a horizontal obstruction.
   - **Mitigation:** Cells must be cleaned (swept, broomed, or vacuumed) before grouting.
   - This is a contractor responsibility before notifying for grout placement (Protocol 121).

3. **Verification of reinforcement bar seating:**
   - Confirm vertical bars sit properly in cells (do not rest on mortar bridges or debris).
   - Gently move bars up and down (small motion only, not dislodging) to check for freedom of movement.

4. **Document cell condition:**
   - Photograph representative cells showing cleanliness.
   - Note any cells requiring cleaning before grout placement.
   - Reference Protocol 121 (Fill Cells) for grout placement procedures.

### Step 11: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire stem wall perimeter and note any anomalies.
2. Verify that all deficiencies identified in steps 1–10 have been logged.
3. Obtain sign-off from contractor that corrections (if any) are complete and ready for grout placement.
4. Photograph:
   - Overall wall elevation (wide shot showing height and length).
   - Block type and course detail (close-up of block and mortar joint).
   - Vertical bar placement with spacing measurement.
   - Lap splice locations with measurement tape.
   - Horizontal reinforcement / bond beam detail with bar size and spacing.
   - Cell cleanliness (representative cells before grout).
   - Anchor bolt placement (if installed).
   - Any openings or header blocks.
   - Any deficiencies or corrections needed.
5. Record all measurements and observations on the field inspection form.
6. Complete escalation checklist (see Section 11).

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All block type, size, and course count correct; mortar joints consistent (3/8 in. ±) and fully bedded; vertical bars correct size, spacing, and lap with footer dowels; bars centered with minimum 1.5 in. cover; horizontal reinforcement / bond beams present at required locations with proper bar size and splices; wall level and plumb within tolerance; cells clean and ready for grout; no active cracks or instability. |
| **HOLD** | Minor corrections needed before grouting: cell cleaning required, minor mortar joint repointing, one or two anchor bolts slightly out of position (correctable before slab), minor plumb/level deviation. Contractor has time to correct on-site. |
| **FAIL** | Critical deficiency: wrong CMU type, wrong bar size/spacing, inadequate lap splice, mortar bridging not cleanable, wall significantly out of plumb/level, missing bond beam or horizontal reinforcement, missing vertical bars, bar cover < 1 in., unauthorized block substitution, wall shifting or cracking. Requires engineer review and potential design modification. |

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Confuses bond beam with lintel.** AI often treats a bond beam course as equivalent to a lintel (a concentrated reinforced element above an opening). These serve different purposes: a bond beam ties the wall together horizontally (distributed load), while a lintel concentrates load above an opening. They have different reinforcement sizing and placement rules. Junior inspectors and some AI models interchange these terms incorrectly.

2. **Assumes knockout/bond beam blocks are automatically open channels.** AI may assume that buying "bond beam block" results in an open channel for the reinforcement. In reality, knockouts must be **actually removed or cut** during block manufacturing or on-site. If standard CMU is installed instead of proper knockout blocks, the wall has no continuous horizontal channel — this is a critical deficiency. Visual verification is required.

3. **Applies concrete column cover rules to masonry walls.** AI often cites 1.5–3 in. cover requirements from ACI 318 (which apply to cast-in-place concrete columns). Masonry wall reinforcement cover per TMS 402/602 is **1.5 in. minimum**, a different standard. Confusing these leads to incorrect acceptance of under-covered bars.

4. **Treats mortar joint quality as irrelevant once cells are grouted.** AI sometimes suggests that mortarjoint thickness and bedding quality don't matter because "the grout will fill voids." In reality, mortar joints carry compression and shear loads between blocks independently of grout. Thin, poorly bedded joints create stress concentrations and reduce wall compressive capacity. Mortar quality is a separate, critical criterion.

5. **Underestimates Florida shearwall reinforcement requirements.** Generic AI responses default to minimum spacing or ignore that Florida's high-velocity hurricane zone (HVHZ) design wind speeds and FBC wind provisions often require **tighter horizontal bar spacing** (e.g., 12 in. or 8 in. O.C.) in shearwall sections than generic IRC minimums. Failure to check the shearwall schedule against the actual wall layout is a common oversight.

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Wrong CMU block type or size (hollow vs. solid, non-loadbearing) | Fail → notify EOR; require block removal and replacement |
| Wrong vertical bar size or count does not match plans | Fail → hold grouting; notify EOR for resolution |
| Vertical bar lap splice < 25 in. or bars not in same cell | Fail → hold grouting; require re-placement or EOR approval |
| Vertical bar cover < 1.5 in. (bars touching block face) | Fail → assess corrective options with EOR (may require bar relocation or accept per design) |
| Mortar joints consistently < 1/4 in. or > 1/2 in. | Hold → require selective repointing of deficient joints before grout |
| Mortar bridging in cells preventing grout flow | Hold → require cell cleaning (sweep, broom, vacuum) before grout placement |
| Missing bond beam course at required location | Fail → notify EOR; determine if design modification or addition of missed course is required |
| Wrong bond beam block type (standard CMU instead of knockout) | Fail → hold grouting; require replacement with proper knockout blocks |
| Horizontal bar splice length < 25 in. for #5 bars | Fail → hold grouting; require bar relocation or lap extension |
| Shearwall bar spacing does not match schedule (bars too far apart) | Fail → hold grouting; require additional bars or EOR modification |
| Wall significantly out of plumb (> 1/2 in. in 10 ft) or shifting | Stop work → notify EOR and structural engineer for assessment; possible shoring or redesign required |
| Active wall cracking, bulging, or instability observed | Stop work → cease construction activity until engineer evaluation complete |
| Cell cleanliness or bridging cannot be remedied on-site | Consult EOR → may require special grout procedures (Protocol 121) or delay grouting |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Overall wall elevation** — wide shot showing full height and length of stem wall from above and side (shows course count and context).
2. **Block type detail** — close-up of CMU block showing manufacturer mark, size, and condition (loadbearing vs. non-loadbearing identification).
3. **Mortar joint detail** — close-up of horizontal and vertical joints with tape measure showing joint thickness (at least 3 different locations).
4. **Full bedding verification** — angle shot showing mortar bed at interior block face (demonstrates no voids or back beveling issues).
5. **Vertical reinforcement placement** — close-up showing vertical bar in cell with ruler showing bar size markings.
6. **Vertical bar spacing** — tape measure spanning distance between two consecutive vertical bars (documents spacing per plans).
7. **Lap splice detail** — tape measure showing full lap length from top of dowel to bottom of vertical bar above (minimum 25 in.).
8. **Lap splice ties** — close-up showing wire ties securing spliced bars together (2 ties minimum).
9. **Vertical bar cover** — tape or measure showing clearance from bar center to block interior face (verify ≥ 1.5 in.).
10. **Horizontal reinforcement / bond beam** — close-up showing bond beam block with knockout/channel visible and horizontal bar in place.
11. **Bond beam bar size and continuity** — tape measure and bar markings to verify bar size and continuous run (splice if present).
12. **Shearwall spacing detail** — tape measure between consecutive horizontal bars in shearwall section (if applicable).
13. **Anchor bolt placement** — showing bolt location, spacing, and vertical alignment (if at this stage).
14. **Wall plumb and level** — level bubble against wall face at multiple heights and overall edge view (demonstrates vertical alignment).
15. **Cell cleanliness** — flashlight shot into representative cells showing absence of mortar droppings, debris, bridging; at least 2 cells photographed.
16. **Cleanout opening** — location and accessibility of cleanout openings in bottom courses (if required).
17. **Openings and headers** — header blocks and lintel placement above openings (if present).
18. **Any deficiencies** — close-up of any issues found (cracks, misalignment, debris) with clear label and reference to location.
19. **Property context** — permit board, address, or site marker in frame to identify project location and date.
20. **Pre-grout overview** — final overview shot after all items inspected, showing wall is ready (or flagged for corrections).
