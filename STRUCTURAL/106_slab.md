# Protocol 106: Slab-on-Grade (SOG) Pre-Pour Inspection

> **Inspection Type:** Structural — Foundation
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — FBC-R, Chapter 5 (Concrete)
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-pour verification of residential slab-on-grade construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after subgrade preparation, vapor barrier placement, and reinforcement placement but before concrete placement**.

### In Scope
- Subgrade / fill compaction and surface condition.
- Vapor barrier material, coverage, overlap, sealing, and damage.
- Reinforcement placement: welded wire fabric (WWF), rebar grid, or fiber mesh (per plans).
- Slab thickness formwork and elevation.
- Under-slab utilities: plumbing rough-in testing, electrical conduit placement.
- Slab-to-stem-wall connections and any thickened edges.
- Expansion and control joint layout.

### Out of Scope
- Concrete mix design verification (batch tickets reviewed at pour).
- Post-pour surface finish or curing verification.
- Structural slab (two-way) design analysis (requires structural engineer).
- Under-slab radon mitigation systems (separate protocol).
- Soil remediation or vapor intrusion testing (requires geotech/environmental specialist).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the slab subgrade, vapor barrier, reinforcement, and utilities are ready for concrete placement by verifying:
- **PASS:** All conditions meet plans and FBC-R requirements; slab is approved for pour.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before pour authorization.
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring repair, replacement, or pour delay.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Do not** walk on placed vapor barrier if avoidable; use temporary plywood walkways.
- **Do not** move, displace, or "test" placed reinforcement.
- **Do not** probe the bearing soil surface with heavy tools that could disturb or compact the prepared subgrade.
- **Do not** enter or disturb any under-slab utility trenches or plumbing sleeves without contractor authorization.
- **Stop condition:** If subgrade shows standing water, soft areas, active groundwater intrusion, or evidence of organic material or fill debris at slab elevation — STOP and notify contractor and site engineer before proceeding.
- **Limitation:** This inspection is a pre-pour condition snapshot. Once the slab is poured, subsurface conditions and vapor barrier are permanently concealed and inaccessible.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Subgrade Preparation and Elevation

1. **Visual inspection of the fill surface:**
   - Confirm the subgrade is clean (no organic material, roots, construction debris, or trash).
   - Verify no standing water or soft/wet areas on the slab area.
   - Check that the surface is visually undisturbed or uniformly compacted (no voids, ruts, or loose soil).

2. **Check compaction records:**
   - If plans require compaction testing (typically 95% Modified Proctor per ASTM D1557), obtain test reports and verify on-site locations.
   - Verify test results meet the specified compaction percentage (95% Modified Proctor is typical for Florida residential).
   - **Note:** ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor) is more stringent than ASTM D698 (Standard Proctor); verify which applies per plans.

3. **Verify elevation and levelness:**
   - Measure the subgrade elevation at multiple points (minimum 4–6 points across the slab area) and compare to the plan finish floor elevation.
   - Use a straightedge (8–10 ft) to check that the subgrade surface is reasonably level within typical tolerance (±0.5 in. over 10 ft is acceptable).
   - Note any slopes or low spots that could trap water under the vapor barrier.

4. **Check for evidence of fill material:**
   - Visually scan the excavation for color changes, loose material, or debris that would indicate unauthorized fill or disturbed soil.
   - If any fill is observed, verify it matches site drawings (material source, depth, compaction) and is properly compacted.

### Step 2: Verify Vapor Barrier Installation

1. **Material verification:**
   - Confirm vapor barrier material per plans (typically 6-mil or 10-mil polyethylene per ASTM D4397).
   - Check that the correct material has been delivered to site (label visible on roll if possible).

2. **Coverage and continuity:**
   - Visually confirm the vapor barrier covers the **entire slab area**.
   - Walk the perimeter and scan the interior to verify no gaps or areas left uncovered.
   - Confirm the barrier is turned up **against the stem wall** on all sides.

3. **Seams and overlaps:**
   - Measure seam overlaps at a minimum of 3 locations.
   - Minimum 6 in. overlap at seams (per FBC-R R506.2.3).
   - If plans require taped or sealed seams, verify tape is applied and seals are continuous.
   - Check that seams are **not** directly under reinforcement (to avoid raised bumps that could affect steel placement).

4. **Inspect for damage:**
   - Look for any visible tears, punctures, holes, or gaps in the vapor barrier.
   - Common damage: cuts from delivery equipment, foot traffic, tool punctures, rebar penetrations.
   - **Any damage must be repaired before pour:** repair method depends on damage size (patches must overlap 6 in. on all sides; large tears may require re-laying section).
   - Verify any repairs are complete and sealed.

5. **Moisture check (if feasible):**
   - If vapor barrier has been in place for >48 hours, a light probe with a non-sharp tool can check for moisture accumulation underneath (condensation).
   - Excessive moisture may indicate inadequate subgrade drainage or a groundwater issue that should be flagged before pour.

### Step 3: Verify Reinforcement Placement and Support

**Critical: This is the #1 field deficiency for slabs.** Reinforcement MUST be elevated in the middle or upper third of the slab, NOT resting on the vapor barrier.

1. **Reinforcement type and specification:**
   - Verify reinforcement type per plans:
     - Welded wire fabric (WWF): typical size 6×6 W1.4×W1.4 for 4 in. residential slab.
     - Deformed rebar: typical #4 or #5 bars in a grid pattern.
     - Fiber mesh: verify this is specified in the mix design (not field-placed); if specified, confirm dosage rate is shown on concrete order.

2. **For welded wire fabric (WWF):**
   - Measure and verify WWF grid spacing (typically 6 in. × 6 in.).
   - **Critical:** Confirm WWF is supported on **chairs, bolsters, or spacers** and is positioned in the **MIDDLE or UPPER THIRD of the slab depth** (for a 4 in. slab, the center of WWF should be 1.5–2.5 in. up from the bottom).
   - Do NOT allow WWF to rest flat on the vapor barrier — this provides essentially zero structural reinforcement.
   - Check that chairs/bolsters are secured and will not shift during the concrete pour.
   - Measure chair spacing along each run (typical spacing 4–6 ft apart) to ensure adequate support.
   - Verify that WWF laps correctly at seams: minimum 2 wire intersections overlap (1 wire × 1 wire grid cell).

3. **For rebar reinforcement:**
   - Verify bar size, spacing, and direction per plans (e.g., #4 bars at 18 in. O.C. in both directions).
   - Check that bars are supported on chairs and positioned in the MIDDLE of the slab.
   - Verify **lap splice lengths:** for #4 bars ≈20 in., for #5 bars ≈25 in. (exact length per plans and ACI 318).
   - Confirm splices are **tied with wire ties** (not just overlapped loosely).
   - Check that no rebar is in contact with the vapor barrier or subgrade.

4. **For fiber mesh:**
   - If fiber mesh (polypropylene or similar) is specified, confirm it is part of the ready-mix **concrete design** (not placed in the field).
   - Fiber mesh provides crack control only; if plans call for WWF or rebar, fiber mesh is **NOT a substitute** for structural reinforcement.
   - Verify the concrete order includes fiber mesh dosage (typical 0.6–1.5 lb/yd³).

### Step 4: Verify Slab Thickness and Elevation

1. **Slab thickness:**
   - Measure at a minimum of 4 locations across the slab area (corners and mid-span).
   - Typical residential: 4 in. thick.
   - Verify formwork, edge screeds, or elevation control is set to the correct thickness.
   - Tolerance: ±0.5 in. is acceptable.

2. **Slab elevation and grade:**
   - Check the finished floor elevation against plans at multiple points.
   - Verify the relationship to stem wall top, grade, and any exterior paving or swales.
   - Confirm screeds are set correctly to achieve the planned slope (if any) for drainage.

3. **Edge conditions:**
   - If plans show thickened slab edges (common at interior bearing walls), verify formwork is in place.
   - Check that the slab edge is properly formed against the stem wall (with any required dowels or keyways in place).

### Step 5: Verify Under-Slab Utilities — Plumbing

1. **Under-slab plumbing rough-in:**
   - Confirm all under-slab plumbing (water, drain, sewer) is installed per the plumbing plan.
   - Verify pipe material (PEX, PVC, copper) and routing against the plumbing plan.

2. **Pressure test documentation:**
   - **Critical:** Obtain the plumbing pressure test report (water or air test) showing passing results.
   - Test should be performed and documented **BEFORE the slab pour**.
   - A plumbing leak discovered after the slab is poured requires cutting and breaking the slab — a very costly repair.
   - Verify test pressure and duration per code and the plumbing inspector's requirements.

3. **Pipe penetrations through vapor barrier:**
   - Confirm all pipe penetrations through the vapor barrier are **sealed** with caulk, foam, or tape.
   - No gaps or holes in the vapor barrier at pipe locations.

4. **Sleeves and future penetrations:**
   - Verify sleeves for future mechanical, electrical, or plumbing penetrations are in place and secured.
   - Sleeves should extend above the slab elevation to avoid water entrapment.

### Step 6: Verify Under-Slab Utilities — Electrical

1. **Electrical conduit routing:**
   - Confirm all under-slab electrical conduit (if any) is installed per the electrical plan.
   - Typical residential slabs may have minimal under-slab electrical, but future penetrations may be stubbed.

2. **Conduit protection:**
   - Verify conduit is secured and protected from displacement during concrete placement.
   - Conduit should be above the vapor barrier on supports if possible, or clamped to reinforcement.

3. **Ufer ground / footer bond:**
   - If required (see [Protocol 200](../ELECTRICAL/200_footer_bond_inspection.md)), confirm any grounding requirements are noted or prepared (typically Ufer ground is in the footer, not the slab).

### Step 7: Verify Expansion and Control Joints

1. **Joint layout and spacing:**
   - Verify expansion/control joint locations match the structural plan or ACI 360R guidance.
   - ACI 360R suggests joint spacing ≈ 1.5× the slab width (e.g., 15–20 ft O.C. for a 10 ft wide slab).
   - Check formwork, tape, or joint control that will define joint locations.

2. **Material and depth:**
   - Confirm joint material per plans (commonly 1/4 in. or 1/2 in. pre-formed joint filler, or saw-cut after pour).
   - If pre-formed material is in place, verify it is intact and positioned at the correct depth.

### Step 8: Verify Slab-to-Stem-Wall Connection

1. **Slab edge against stem wall:**
   - Confirm the slab edge is properly formed or constructed against the stem wall.
   - Check for any required **dowels** or **keyways** per structural plans that tie the slab to the stem wall.
   - If dowels are required, verify size, spacing, and projection into both the slab and stem wall.

2. **Thickened slab edges:**
   - If plans show thickened edges at bearing walls, verify formwork is in place for the thickened section.
   - Confirm reinforcement in thickened edge is correctly placed.

### Step 9: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. **Complete a perimeter and full-area walkthrough** and note any anomalies or areas of concern.

2. **Photograph:**
   - Overall slab area (wide shot showing vapor barrier, reinforcement, and any utilities).
   - Vapor barrier coverage (close-ups of seams, overlaps, and any damage).
   - Reinforcement placement and support (close-ups showing height above vapor barrier, chair spacing, lap splices).
   - Under-slab utilities: plumbing layout, pressure test report, electrical conduit.
   - Subgrade condition and elevation.
   - Slab-to-stem-wall connection.
   - Any deficiencies or corrective work.

3. **Record all measurements and observations** on the field inspection form.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | Subgrade compacted and level per plans; vapor barrier fully installed with 6 in. overlaps and no damage; reinforcement properly elevated on supports in middle/upper third of slab; all utilities installed and tested; slab thickness and elevation correct; no deficiencies. |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable on-site before pour (e.g., isolated vapor barrier puncture needing patch, one chair needing adjustment, seam tape needing completion, isolated debris needing removal). |
| **FAIL** | Reinforcement resting on vapor barrier; subgrade with standing water or soft fill; vapor barrier missing or severely damaged; compaction test results below specification; plumbing not pressure-tested; utilities conflicting with slab; slab thickness significantly under plan; deficiencies requiring major rework before pour. |

---

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **#1 Error: Advises placing WWF directly on the ground.** The most common field deficiency is WWF resting flat on the vapor barrier, providing essentially zero structural reinforcement. WWF **must be elevated on chairs or supports** to be positioned in the **middle or upper third of the slab**. This is non-negotiable for structural performance. LLMs frequently miss this critical requirement.

2. **Treats vapor barrier as optional or "recommended."** FBC-R R506.2.3 **requires** a vapor retarder under residential concrete slabs. LLMs often present it as optional or advisory rather than code-mandated.

3. **Ignores under-slab plumbing verification.** AI commonly omits the requirement to obtain **pressure test results before the pour**. A plumbing leak discovered after the slab is poured requires breaking the slab — a catastrophic and expensive failure. This is a critical inspection task that AI frequently overlooks.

4. **Cites wrong compaction standard.** LLMs often reference ASTM D698 (Standard Proctor) when Florida specs typically require ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor) at 95%. These are different tests with different compaction values. Verify the correct standard is specified in plans or geotechnical report.

5. **Suggests fiber mesh is equivalent to WWF.** Fiber mesh (polypropylene fibers in the concrete mix) provides crack control only and is **NOT a structural reinforcement** in place of WWF or rebar. If plans specify WWF or rebar, fiber mesh cannot substitute.

6. **Misses vapor barrier damage and sealing issues.** AI often treats vapor barrier as a simple commodity without addressing overlaps, sealing, damage detection, and repair protocols. Vapor barrier integrity is critical to limiting moisture migration into the slab.

- [ ] Plan sheet reference (slab/foundation plan revision and date).
- [ ] Subgrade elevation measurements (minimum 4–6 points).
- [ ] Compaction test report(s): test locations, results, percentage of Modified Proctor.
- [ ] Vapor barrier material type, coverage verification, seam overlap measurements (minimum 3 locations).
- [ ] Vapor barrier damage assessment and any repairs documented.
- [ ] Reinforcement type, size, spacing, support height above vapor barrier (WWF chair spacing or rebar elevation measurements).
- [ ] Lap splice length verification (minimum 3 locations if applicable).
- [ ] Under-slab plumbing pressure test report: test method, pressure, duration, pass/fail result.
- [ ] Electrical conduit routing and protection assessment.
- [ ] Slab thickness measurements (minimum 4 points) and formwork elevation check.
- [ ] Expansion/control joint layout verification.
- [ ] Slab-to-stem-wall connection detail and any dowel/keyway verification.
- [ ] Photo set with scale reference (minimum 12 photos per inspection).
- [ ] Deficiency list (if any) with required corrective actions and contractor sign-off on corrections.
- [ ] Final disposition: PASS / HOLD / FAIL with PE signature and date.
- [ ] **Limitations statement:** "This inspection is limited to visual observation of accessible, exposed conditions at the time of inspection. Once concrete is placed, subgrade conditions, vapor barrier, and under-slab utilities are permanently concealed and inaccessible."

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Reinforcement (WWF/rebar) resting on vapor barrier | FAIL → require lifting onto chairs; do not pour |
| Vapor barrier with tears, punctures, or missing sections | HOLD → require repair or re-laying; inspect repair before proceed |
| Subgrade with standing water or soft/wet areas | HOLD → assess drainage, evaluate fill replacement; notify geotechnical engineer if severe |
| Subgrade evidence of organic fill or construction debris | FAIL → require removal and replacement with clean fill; notify EOR |
| Compaction test results below specification (e.g., <95% Modified Proctor) | HOLD → require re-compaction and re-test; notify site engineer |
| Under-slab plumbing pressure test **not passed** or **not documented** | STOP WORK → plumbing must be tested and passing before pour; pressure test report required on site |
| Under-slab utility conflict (pipe/conduit crossing slab edge) | HOLD → coordinate with contractors; modify routing or plan per EOR |
| Slab thickness significantly under plan (e.g., 3 in. instead of 4 in.) | FAIL → adjust formwork to correct thickness before pour |
| Slab elevation variance >1 in. from plan | HOLD → assess impact on finished floor, exterior drainage, and stem wall interface; adjust screeds if necessary |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Subgrade overview** — wide shot of entire slab area showing fill surface condition and elevation.
2. **Subgrade detail** — close-up of bearing surface showing clean soil condition and no debris.
3. **Vapor barrier wide shot** — interior of slab showing full barrier coverage and any visible seams.
4. **Vapor barrier seams** — close-up of seam overlap with tape measure showing 6+ in. overlap.
5. **Vapor barrier edge** — corner showing barrier turned up against stem wall.
6. **Vapor barrier damage (if any)** — close-up of any tears, patches, or repairs with clear identification.
7. **Reinforcement placement (WWF)** — close-up showing WWF elevated on chairs with measurement indicating height above vapor barrier.
8. **Reinforcement placement (rebar)** — close-up showing rebar grid spacing and support elevation (if applicable).
9. **Chair/support detail** — close-up showing rebar chair or bolster and securing method.
10. **Under-slab utilities** — overview of plumbing and electrical routing before burial.
11. **Plumbing pressure test documentation** — photo of test report showing date, method, pressure, and PASS result.
12. **Slab-to-stem-wall** — detail shot showing slab edge, any dowels, and connection to stem wall.
13. **Formwork and elevation** — showing screeds, edge forms, and thickness indicators.
14. **Joint preparation (if applicable)** — showing expansion/control joint material or layout.
15. **Overall site context** — property, permit board, or address marker in frame.
