# Protocol 302: Plumbing 2nd Rough (Top-Out) — Above-Slab Rough-In Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Plumbing — Above-Slab Rough-In (2nd Rough / Top-Out)
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Plumbing Code (FPC), 8th Edition / Florida Building Code (FBC) — Plumbing Volume
> **Author/Reviewer PE:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Above-slab plumbing rough-in verification for single-family residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after framing is complete and plumbing is roughed-in through walls and ceiling, but before drywall is hung**. This is the final opportunity to inspect DWV, vent, and water supply systems before concealment.

### In Scope
- All drain, waste, and vent (DWV) piping above slab: sizing, fittings, orientation, support, and joint integrity.
- Vent system: individual and common vents, rise height above flood level rim, termination location and clearances, absence of sags or crown venting.
- Water supply distribution above slab: pipe material (copper Type L or PEX), sizing, hot/cold separation, stub-out locations, anti-scald protection, support, and isolation from dissimilar metals.
- Water heater rough-in (if applicable at this stage): location, platform elevation, T&P relief valve discharge piping.
- Pressure test results: water supply system at 80 PSI for 15 minutes (or per AHJ), DWV test per FPC 312.
- Firestopping at rated wall/ceiling assemblies (coordination with framing/fireproofing trade).

### Out of Scope
- Underground rough-in or below-slab piping (covered under Protocol 301 — 1st Rough).
- Drywall installation, interior finishes, or trim work.
- Coordination with mechanical (HVAC duct clearances) — separate inspection.
- Water softener, greywater, or specialty systems (if not on base plans).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether above-slab plumbing is ready for concealment by verifying:
- **PASS:** All DWV, vent, and water supply systems meet plans and FPC; pressure tests pass; no deficiencies observed.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before drywall approval (e.g., missing water hammer arrestor, unsupported pipe segment, vent termination detail pending roof coordination).
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies that violate code or plans and must be corrected before concealment (e.g., wrong pipe size, crown venting, missing trap seal protection, improper vent termination height).

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Do not** cut, disturb, or remove any piping to verify routing — rely on visual tracing and plans.
- **Do not** perform pressure testing yourself — observe and document contractor's or plumber's test under supervision.
- **Do not** enter wall cavities or crawlspaces without adequate ventilation and clearance.
- **Do not** damage drywall or framing to gain access — if concealed routing cannot be visually verified, note as HOLD pending contractor confirmation.
- **Electrical clearance:** Maintain minimum 12 in. clearance between water supply lines and electrical wiring per FPC 606.3. Do not touch live electrical wiring.
- **Fall/slip prevention:** Use ladders for high roof or attic vent terminations; do not reach beyond safe extension.
- **Pressure test observation:** Stay clear of gauge and piping during pressurization. If any hissing, spray, or unusual sounds occur during test, STOP immediately and alert contractor.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify DWV Pipe Sizing and Fixture Unit Loads

1. Obtain the plumbing plans and locate the fixture schedule (typical fixtures: 2–3 baths, kitchen, laundry, other).
2. For each drain line segment, verify pipe size per FPC Chapter 7:
   - Toilet drains: minimum 3 in. (gravity).
   - Bathroom sink/vanity: minimum 1.5 in.
   - Shower/tub: minimum 2 in. (combined drain and overflow).
   - Kitchen sink: minimum 1.5 in. (trap arm); 2 in. if dishwasher connected.
3. Confirm total fixture unit load on each stack or branch does not exceed pipe capacity:
   - Example: 3 in. vertical stack = up to 48 fixture units (FPC Table 703.2).
4. If a stack or branch appears oversized or undersized relative to fixtures served, request plumbing contractor to justify sizing per FPC.

### Step 2: Verify DWV Fitting Types and Orientation

1. **Long-turn fittings in horizontal runs:** Confirm that long-sweep 90° elbows (or longer-turn fittings) are used for changes of direction in horizontal DWV piping, not short-turn 90° elbows.
2. **Sanitary tees (vertical-to-horizontal transitions):**
   - Sanitary tees must be installed in vertical orientation (top branch on vertical rise, horizontal outlet to side).
   - **Red flag:** Sanitary tees laid flat (horizontal-to-horizontal) create pockets that collect debris. This is a common deficiency.
3. **No crown venting:** Verify that vent takeoffs are NOT connected to the crown (top) of the trap weir. Vent connection must be on the fixture drain downstream of the trap, per FPC 906.1.
4. **Trap installation:** Confirm that each fixture has an accessible, properly pitched trap with a visible trap seal (typically 2 in. water depth).

### Step 3: Verify Waste Arm Lengths and Vent Distances

1. For each fixture connected to a toilet stack or branch, measure the **trap arm** (horizontal distance from the trap outlet to the vent connection or stack).
2. Refer to FPC Table 1002.1:
   - 1.5 in. trap arm: maximum 2.5 ft to vent.
   - 2 in. trap arm: maximum 3.5 ft to vent.
   - 3 in. trap arm: maximum 6 ft to vent.
3. If trap arm exceeds maximum distance, the trap seal will siphon — FAIL condition. Contractor must add a vent or reduce trap arm length.

### Step 4: Verify PVC Joint Integrity (Primer and Cement)

1. Visually inspect all PVC DWV joints for evidence of:
   - **Purple primer visible:** All joints must show purple primer residue on the pipe exterior (indicates pipes were primed per manufacturer spec).
   - **Cement evidence:** Joints should show cement residue (white, tacky appearance if recently glued). Dry, clean joints without evidence of cement are suspect.
2. Check for **cold joints** (joints assembled without proper priming/cementing) — they appear clean, dry, with no purple or white residue.
3. **Red flag:** Any cold joints found = FAIL. Contractor must break joint, reprep with primer/cement, and reassemble.

### Step 5: Verify Pipe Support and Spacing

1. **Horizontal PVC DWV support:**
   - 1.5–2 in.: maximum 4 ft between supports (hangers, clamps).
   - 3–4 in.: maximum 4 ft between supports (FPC 308.4).
2. Verify that supports are:
   - Mounted to framing (studs, bands, plates), not hanging or sagging.
   - Non-corrosive (plastic or stainless steel clamps, not bare steel or copper wire).
   - Positioned to allow pipe to expand/contract without binding.
3. Check that no long horizontal runs have **sags or low points** that could collect water and block airflow (especially vent lines).

### Step 6: Verify Vent System Completeness and Rise Height

1. **Fixture vent assignment:** Walk through each fixture (toilet, sink, tub, shower) and visually trace the vent line:
   - Confirm each fixture has an assigned vent (individual, common, wet vent, or circuit vent per design).
2. **Vent rise above flood level rim (FLR):**
   - Measure the vertical distance from the point where the vent line leaves the fixture drain to where it turns horizontal or exits the building.
   - **Code requirement:** Vent must rise a minimum of 6 in. above the flood level rim of the highest fixture served before going horizontal (FPC 906.1).
   - **Common deficiency:** Vents that barely clear a vanity counter or that turn horizontal immediately at ceiling height. Measure to confirm minimum 6 in. clearance.
3. **Crown vent check:** Reconfirm no vent is connected at the crown (top) of the trap weir. Crown venting siphons the trap seal.

### Step 7: Verify Vent Termination Location and Height

1. **Roof penetration and height (FPC 906.2):**
   - All vent stacks must terminate above the roof surface:
     - Minimum **6 in. above roof** (standard).
     - Minimum **12 in. above roof** if within 10 ft of any vertical opening (window, door, deck railing, air intake).
2. Measure the vertical rise of the vent termination above the roofline. Common deficiency: vents that just barely clear roof flashing.
3. **Clearances from adjacent features:**
   - No vent termination within 3 ft of a window or skylight (FPC 906.2(1)).
   - No vent within 10 ft of an air intake (fresh air inlet for HVAC) — or 3 ft if higher by 12 in. (FPC 906.2(2)).
4. **Vent cap or termination:** Confirm vent has a cap or termination fitting (not an open pipe); cap must allow air to escape (no screen that would clog).
5. **Roof flashing coordination:** If vent termination detail is not yet finalized (roofing trade not started), note as HOLD pending roofing coordination.

### Step 8: Verify Water Supply Pipe Material and Sizing

1. **Pipe material verification:**
   - Check all exposed water supply piping: confirm material matches plans (copper Type L or PEX per ASTM F876/F877).
   - **Copper:** Confirm proper color (reddish, not green oxidation if old copper).
   - **PEX:** Confirm color coding: red for hot, blue for cold, white or natural for water heater discharge (per plan or local convention).
2. **Pipe sizing per FPC Chapter 6:**
   - Main water supply line: typically 3/4 in. or 1 in. (verify per plans and fixture unit demand).
   - Hot water distribution: typically 1/2 in. or 5/8 in.
   - Individual fixture supply lines: 1/2 in. or 3/8 in. per fixture (verify plans).
3. Check for undersizing or oversizing relative to fixtures served. Request justification if sizing appears inconsistent with FPC 603 demand calculation.

### Step 9: Verify Hot and Cold Separation and Stub-Out Locations

1. **Hot and cold line separation:** Confirm hot and cold lines are clearly separated and not running in the same raceway or bundle (unless insulated per plan).
2. **Stub-out locations:** Walk to each fixture location (bathroom, kitchen, laundry) and verify:
   - Water supply stub-outs are present at the correct wall location per plans.
   - Stub-outs are capped or valved (to prevent contamination).
   - Stub-out height and location match fixture connection points (e.g., vanity hot/cold inlets, kitchen faucet connection height).
3. **Identify any missing or mislocated stub-outs** — these must be corrected before drywall.

### Step 10: Verify Anti-Scald Protection (Thermostatic Mixing Valves)

1. **Shower/tub anti-scald requirements (FPC 422):**
   - Every shower and tub must be protected by a thermostat mixing valve (TMV) or pressure-balance valve (PBV) that limits outlet temperature to a maximum 120°F.
2. Locate the mixing valve(s):
   - Typically installed at the water heater or at the shower/tub valve itself.
   - Confirm the valve is rated for the intended use (shower valve, water heater protection, or combination).
3. Verify the valve is **correctly oriented** per manufacturer specs (often has arrows showing hot/cold inlet and mixed outlet).
4. **Red flag:** Absence of mixing valve on a shower = FAIL. Contractor must install before rough approval.

### Step 11: Verify Water Hammer Arrestors and Quick-Closing Valve Protection

1. **Water hammer arrestor locations (FPC 606.4):**
   - Quick-closing valves require protection against water hammer:
     - Washing machine inlet (solenoid fill valve).
     - Dishwasher inlet (solenoid valve).
     - Any other quick-closing valve (icemaker inlet, automatic flush valve, etc.).
2. Locate arrestors:
   - Typically air-charged or spring-loaded devices installed near the valve.
   - Should be visible and labeled.
3. **Red flag:** Washing machine or dishwasher connected without visible arrestor = FAIL. Contractor must install before test.

### Step 12: Verify Pipe Support and Material Isolation

1. **Copper pipe support (ASTM B88):**
   - Maximum spacing: 6 ft for 3/4 in. and smaller, 10 ft for 1 in. and larger (typical — verify manufacturer specs).
   - Supports must be non-corrosive (plastic, brass, or stainless steel — not bare iron or galvanized).
2. **PEX pipe support (per manufacturer specs):**
   - Typically 32 in. maximum spacing for vertical runs, 3 ft maximum for horizontal.
   - Verify manufacturer installation manual on-site.
3. **Isolation from dissimilar metals:**
   - Confirm copper piping does not touch steel framing studs, beams, or fasteners directly (common in wood-framed construction with steel studs).
   - If contact occurs, require dielectric isolation (insulation sleeves, washers, or separation).

### Step 13: Verify Water Heater Rough-In (if at this stage)

1. **Location and platform elevation:**
   - Confirm water heater location matches plans.
   - If located in a garage, verify platform elevation: **minimum 18 in. above floor per FPC 504.6** (to keep ignition source away from hazardous vapors).
2. **T&P (Temperature & Pressure) Relief Valve discharge piping (FPC 504.5):**
   - Locate the T&P valve and its discharge line.
   - **Discharge termination:** Pipe must terminate within **6 in. of the floor** or to an **exterior drain** (no upward discharge into ceiling space).
   - **No size reduction:** Discharge pipe must be the same size as the T&P outlet (no reducing couplers).
   - **No threading:** Discharge pipe cannot have threads after the T&P valve (no adapters or fittings to connect future lines).
   - **No traps:** Discharge line cannot have a trap or siphon bend.
   - **No future connections:** Line terminates at floor drain, sump, or exterior — it is not used for other purposes.
3. **Drain pan (if in attic or elevated location):** Confirm pan is installed with drain line terminating to exterior per FPC 504.6.

### Step 14: Verify Pressure Test (Water Supply System)

1. **Test observation:** The contractor or licensed plumber must perform the water supply pressure test in your presence.
2. **Test parameters (FPC 312):**
   - Pressure: **80 PSI minimum, 100 PSI maximum** for 15 minutes (or per AHJ variance).
   - All water supply piping (copper, PEX) must be isolated and pressurized.
3. **During test:**
   - **Pressure gauge must be visible and readable throughout the 15-minute period.**
   - No pressure drop is acceptable — if gauge needle drops, that indicates a leak.
   - **FAIL if:** Pressure drops below 80 PSI at any point during the 15-minute hold.
4. **If pressure drops:** Contractor must locate and repair the leak, re-pressurize, and retest. Do not sign off until test passes.
5. **Document:** Photograph gauge at start, middle, and end of test. Record final pressure reading and time on inspection form.

### Step 15: Verify DWV System Pressure or Water Test (FPC 312)

1. **Test type:** Contractor will perform either a water test or air test per FPC 312. Water test is typical for rough-in.
2. **Water test method:**
   - Fill the system with water to the overflow/high point.
   - Hold water level for 15 minutes (or per AHJ).
   - No water level drop indicates PASS; any drop indicates a leak.
3. **Air test method (alternative):**
   - Pressurize system to 5 PSI.
   - Hold for 15 minutes.
   - Gauge pressure must not drop.
4. **Observe the test:** Be present to witness contractor performing test. No leaks are acceptable.
5. **Document:** Record test type, duration, pressure (if air test), and result (PASS/FAIL).

### Step 16: Verify Firestopping at Rated Assemblies

1. **Fire-rated wall/ceiling penetrations:** If any plumbing penetrates a fire-rated wall assembly (e.g., 1-hour wall between garage and living space), verify that:
   - Penetrations are properly sealed or firestopped per the fire-rating design.
   - Firestopping material is installed per manufacturer specs (caulk, sleeves, putty pads, etc.).
2. **Coordination:** Firestopping may be deferred to a later trade (fireproofing contractor). If not yet complete, note as HOLD pending fireproofing sign-off.

### Step 17: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire above-slab plumbing system (visible portions) and note any anomalies or deficiencies.
2. Verify that rough pressure tests are complete and passed.
3. Photograph:
   - Overall DWV system (wide shots showing major runs and stacks).
   - PVC joint details (showing purple primer and cement evidence).
   - Trap and vent configuration for primary fixtures (toilet, sinks, tub).
   - Vent rise above flood level rim at one or more fixtures.
   - Water supply stub-outs at kitchen and bathroom locations.
   - Mixing valve and any anti-scald device.
   - Water heater rough-in (if applicable) and T&P discharge detail.
   - Pressure gauge at test (before, during, after).
   - Pipe support and spacing details.
   - Any deficiencies or red flags.
4. Record all observations on the field inspection form.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All DWV sizing and fittings correct; all joints properly primed/cemented; waste arm lengths compliant; vents rise above FLR and terminate at code height; water supply sized per plans with proper stub-outs and anti-scald protection; water hammer arrestors present; support spacing correct; pressure tests passed without leaks; no crown venting or improper fitting orientation. |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable before drywall (e.g., water hammer arrestor not yet installed but pipe ready, vent termination detail pending roof coordination, one stub-out slightly mislocated but correctable, PVC joint primer/cement confirmation pending contractor re-work). |
| **FAIL** | Wrong pipe size, wrong fitting type (short-turn in horizontal, sanitary tee on back), crown vent detected, waste arm exceeds max distance, no vent assigned to a fixture, vent termination height below 6 in. above roof, missing or inadequate mixing valve, no water hammer protection, pressure test shows leaks or drop below 80 PSI, DWV test fails, cold joints on PVC, improper support spacing, water heater T&P discharge piping incorrect or missing. |

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Vent termination height — AI omits the 12 in. rule for openings.** LLMs frequently say "vents must be 6 in. above the roof" without mentioning the critical FPC 906.2(2) requirement: **12 in. above roof if within 10 ft of any vertical opening** (window, door, deck rail, air intake). AI defaults to the minimum 6 in. and misses rooftop vent stacks that barely clear flashing but fail the 10 ft opening clearance rule.

2. **Allows sanitary tees on their back (horizontal-to-horizontal).** AI often shows sanitary tees installed flat in horizontal runs. Sanitary tees are designed for vertical-to-horizontal transitions. Laying them flat creates a pocket that collects debris and blocks flow. This is a fundamental DWV design error that AI misses because it doesn't visually model the interior water path of the fitting.

3. **Crown venting is not flagged as a deficiency.** AI responds to "where should the vent connect?" with "at the top of the trap" or "at the highest point." Crown venting (vent takeoff from the top of the trap weir) violates FPC 906.1 and siphons the trap seal. AI doesn't recognize this because it conflates "high" (physically above) with "correct" (downstream of trap seal).

4. **T&P relief valve discharge piping is incomplete or wrong.** AI often says "install a T&P valve" without addressing the discharge line. The discharge piping must (per FPC 504.5): terminate within 6 in. of the floor or to exterior, cannot be reduced in size, cannot have threads after the valve, cannot have a trap, and cannot discharge upward. AI treats T&P valve installation as complete without these discharge requirements.

5. **Water hammer arrestors are omitted or treated as optional.** FPC 606.4 requires protection against water hammer for quick-closing valves (washing machines, dishwashers, automatic valves). AI often lists these fixtures without specifying arrestor requirements, treating the protection as a "nice to have" rather than code-mandated.

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Pressure test shows drop below 80 PSI or leaks visible | FAIL — hold drywall; contractor must locate/repair leak and retest |
| DWV water or air test fails (water level drop or pressure drop) | FAIL — hold drywall; contractor must locate/repair leak and retest |
| Vent termination height below 6 in. above roof, or below 12 in. within 10 ft of opening | FAIL — contractor must extend vent or relocate (rare, typically requires design change) |
| Crown venting detected, or sanitary tee oriented flat (horizontal-to-horizontal) | FAIL — contractor must break joint and reorient fitting or relocate vent connection |
| Waste arm exceeds FPC Table 1002.1 distance to vent | FAIL — contractor must add intermediate vent or reduce trap arm length |
| Missing mixing valve on shower/tub, or incorrect valve type | FAIL — contractor must install anti-scald protection before test |
| Missing water hammer arrestor on washing machine, dishwasher, or quick-closing valve | FAIL — contractor must install arrestor before rough sign-off |
| T&P relief discharge piping incorrect (reduced, threaded, trapped, upward discharge) | FAIL — contractor must corrected discharge piping per FPC 504.5 |
| PVC DWV cold joints found (no primer/cement evidence) | FAIL — contractor must break joint, reprep with primer/cement, and reassemble; retest system |
| Pipe support spacing exceeds code maximum (4 ft for 1.5–2 in. horizontal DWV, 4 ft for 3–4 in. horizontal DWV) | HOLD — contractor must add support hangers before drywall (non-load-bearing correction) |
| Copper piping in direct contact with steel framing (no dielectric isolation) | HOLD — contractor must isolate with sleeves, washers, or separation (galvanic corrosion prevention) |
| Firestopping at rated penetrations not installed | HOLD — defer to fireproofing trade; coordinate completion date |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Wide shot of DWV system** — overall view of main stack(s) and horizontal runs showing layout.
2. **Main toilet stack detail** — close-up showing stack size, fixture connections, and routing.
3. **PVC joint detail** — close-up of a glued joint showing purple primer and/or cement residue.
4. **Sanitary tee orientation** — detail showing vertical-to-horizontal transition with correct orientation (not flat/back).
5. **Trap and vent configuration** — detail showing fixture trap, trap arm, and vent connection (verify no crown vent).
6. **Vent rise above FLR** — measurement of vent line vertical rise above the fixture's flood level rim (confirm minimum 6 in.).
7. **Roof vent termination (exterior shot if accessible)** — showing vent clearance above roof surface and distance to nearby openings; measure height.
8. **Water supply stub-outs** — detail at bathroom vanity and kitchen sink showing hot/cold inlets and rough condition.
9. **Mixing valve or anti-scald device** — close-up showing valve type, inlet/outlet orientation, and installation location.
10. **Water heater T&P relief discharge** — detail showing discharge line termination, pipe size, and route to floor drain or exterior.
11. **Pressure test gauge during test** — photograph at start, middle, and end of 15-minute hold showing pressure reading.
12. **DWV water test or air test setup** — showing test configuration and any monitoring devices.
13. **Pipe support and spacing** — detail showing hanger spacing, clamp type (non-corrosive), and proper routing through framing.
14. **Any deficiencies** — close-up photographs of any issues found with clear identification and measurement reference (tape, ruler).
15. **Overview with context** — wide shot including framing, visible equipment, and property address or permit board if visible.
