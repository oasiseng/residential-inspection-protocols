# Protocol 303: Sewer — Building Sewer and Septic System Connection Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Plumbing — Building Sewer, Septic Tank, and Drainfield Connection
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Plumbing Code (FPC), 8th Edition; Florida Building Code (FBC) — Plumbing Volume; FAC 64E-6 (Standards for Onsite Sewage Treatment and Disposal Systems)
> **Author/Reviewer:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-04-09

---

## 1) Scope

Verification of the building sewer from the foundation wall exit to the point of disposal — including the building sewer lateral, septic tank installation, distribution box (if present), and drainfield connection. This protocol covers **new construction and replacement systems** inspected before backfill and before the system is placed in service.

### In Scope
- Building sewer pipe from the building drain exit at the foundation perimeter to the septic tank inlet.
- Pipe material, sizing, slope, joint integrity, and bedding for the building sewer lateral.
- Cleanout locations along the building sewer (at the building, at changes of direction, and at required intervals).
- Backwater valve installation and orientation (if required by plans or local AHJ).
- Septic tank: installation depth, orientation, inlet/outlet elevation differential, structural condition, watertightness.
- Outlet baffle and effluent filter (if required by permit or FAC 64E-6).
- Distribution box (D-box): level setting, inlet/outlet pipe connections, structural condition.
- Drainfield (absorption field): trench dimensions, aggregate bedding, perforated pipe layout, filter fabric, separation from seasonal high water table (SHWT).
- Pipe connections between septic tank outlet, D-box, and drainfield laterals.
- Setback distances from well, property lines, water bodies, and structures per FAC 64E-6.

### Out of Scope
- Under-slab plumbing rough-in (covered under [Protocol 301](./301_1st_rough.md)).
- Above-slab plumbing rough-in (covered under [Protocol 302](./302_2nd_rough.md)).
- Municipal sewer connection (tap and lateral to public main) — different inspection; this protocol is septic only.
- Septic system design (sizing, soil testing, mound system design) — requires licensed septic contractor and FDOH permit.
- Pump chamber, dosing pump, or advanced treatment unit (ATU) commissioning — requires manufacturer and FDOH sign-off.
- Grease trap or commercial interceptor inspection.

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the building sewer and septic system installation is ready for backfill and service by verifying:
- **PASS:** All piping, septic tank, distribution, and drainfield components meet plans, FPC, and FAC 64E-6; pressure test (if applicable) passes; setbacks verified.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before backfill authorization (e.g., one cleanout cap missing, minor slope adjustment, D-box leveling).
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring repair, replacement, or redesign before backfill (e.g., tank damage, setback violation, drainfield elevation below SHWT).

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Trench safety:** Do not enter excavations deeper than 4 ft without shoring or sloping per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P. Inspect from the edge where possible.
- **Do not enter the septic tank** under any circumstances — confined space with toxic and explosive gas hazard (hydrogen sulfide, methane). Inspect from the access opening only.
- **Do not disturb placed piping or tank** during inspection — no unauthorized moving, prying, or pressure application beyond the approved test protocol.
- **Do not apply loads** to drainfield area (no vehicle traffic, no heavy equipment on drainfield trenches or within 10 ft of the drainfield perimeter).
- **Stop condition:** If trenches show active groundwater at or above the pipe or aggregate elevation, STOP — this may indicate seasonal high water table (SHWT) conflict. Notify FDOH and septic designer immediately.
- **Stop condition:** If the septic tank shows visible cracking, displacement, or evidence of flotation during or after placement, STOP — structural integrity compromised.
- **Biological hazard:** If inspecting a replacement system where an existing tank is being reused or demolished, treat all contents and surfaces as biohazardous. Do not make direct contact.

## 6) Preconditions

- [ ] Approved FDOH onsite sewage permit is on-site with the site plan showing setbacks, tank size, and drainfield layout.
- [ ] Building permit plumbing plans (sealed) are on-site and match the FDOH permit.
- [ ] Building sewer trench is excavated and piping is installed but not yet backfilled.
- [ ] Septic tank is set in the excavation and is accessible for inspection (lid(s) open or removable).
- [ ] Distribution box (if present) is set and accessible.
- [ ] Drainfield trenches are excavated to design depth, aggregate is placed, and perforated pipe is laid but not yet covered.
- [ ] Licensed septic contractor is on-site or available by phone.
- [ ] FDOH inspector coordination: confirm whether FDOH requires a separate inspection or will accept the engineer/building inspector report.
- [ ] All solvent-weld joints on the building sewer are fully cured (minimum 24 hours).

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Setback Distances

1. Using the FDOH-approved site plan, measure and confirm the following **minimum setback distances** per FAC 64E-6.005:

   | Feature | From Septic Tank | From Drainfield | From D-Box |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | Private well (potable) | 75 ft | 75 ft | 75 ft |
   | Public well | 100 ft | 100 ft | 100 ft |
   | Property line | 5 ft | 5 ft | 5 ft |
   | Building foundation | 5 ft | 5 ft | 5 ft |
   | Swimming pool | 15 ft | 15 ft | 15 ft |
   | Surface water body (lake, canal, retention pond) | 75 ft | 75 ft | 75 ft |
   | Stormwater retention area | 15 ft | 15 ft | 15 ft |
   | Non-potable well | 50 ft | 50 ft | 50 ft |

   > **Note:** These are minimum values from FAC 64E-6. Local AHJ may impose greater distances. Always verify against the approved FDOH permit, which may specify site-specific setbacks.

2. Measure from the **nearest edge** of the component (tank wall, drainfield trench edge, D-box wall) to the feature.
3. **Red flag:** Any setback measurement that is less than the permitted distance = **FAIL / STOP WORK**. This is a permit violation and requires FDOH notification.

![Measuring setback distance from septic tank to property line with tape](images/303_setback_measurement.jpg)

### Step 2: Verify Building Sewer Pipe — Material, Sizing, and Slope

1. Walk the entire building sewer run from the foundation wall exit to the septic tank inlet:
   - **Pipe material:** PVC Schedule 40 (ASTM D2665) or PVC SDR 35 (ASTM D3034). SDR 35 is the most common for building sewers in Florida residential.
   - **Pipe size:** Minimum 4 in. diameter for building sewer (FPC 704.1). Verify against plans.
   - Confirm all pipe markings are visible (manufacturer, ASTM standard, size, schedule/SDR).

2. Check **slope** using a 4 ft level or digital slope gauge:
   - **Minimum slope for 4 in. building sewer:** 1/8 in. per foot (FPC 704.3).
   - Measure slope at a minimum of 3 locations along the run.
   - Slope must be **continuous and downward toward the septic tank** — no bellies, sags, or backslope.

3. Check for **uniform bedding and support:**
   - Pipe must rest on compacted bedding (sand or pea gravel), not on rocks, debris, or uneven soil.
   - No deflection or visible sag between support points.

4. Verify **fittings:**
   - Only long-turn (long-sweep) fittings for changes of direction in horizontal runs.
   - No short-turn 90° elbows in horizontal building sewer.
   - 45° bends or two 22.5° bends preferred for directional changes.

![Building sewer pipe in trench showing slope, SDR 35 marking, and bedding](images/303_building_sewer_pipe_trench.jpg)

![Long-sweep fitting at change of direction in building sewer](images/303_long_sweep_fitting.jpg)

### Step 3: Verify Building Sewer Joint Integrity

1. **PVC solvent-weld joints:**
   - All joints must show **visible purple primer** on the outside of the fitting socket and pipe exterior (Florida requirement).
   - Joint surfaces must be smooth, fully seated (pipe fully inserted), no gaps or cracks.
   - No visible cement drip obscuring the joint assessment.

2. **Gasketed joints (SDR 35):**
   - If using bell-and-spigot gasketed pipe (common for SDR 35), verify:
     - Gasket is properly seated in the bell.
     - Spigot is fully inserted to the reference mark.
     - No visible displacement or gap at the joint.
   - Check that gasket material is elastomeric and not dried, cracked, or missing.

3. Photograph representative joints (minimum 3 along the run).

![Close-up of PVC joint showing purple primer on building sewer](images/303_sewer_joint_primer.jpg)

![SDR 35 gasketed joint showing full insertion to reference mark](images/303_gasketed_joint_detail.jpg)

### Step 4: Verify Cleanout Locations

1. Confirm cleanouts are installed per FPC 708.1:
   - **At the building:** Cleanout within 5 ft of the building foundation (exterior side, where the building sewer exits).
   - **At changes of direction:** Cleanout at each change of direction exceeding 45°.
   - **At intervals:** Cleanout at maximum 100 ft intervals along the building sewer.

2. Verify cleanout **accessibility after backfill:**
   - Cleanout must extend to grade level or be housed in a valve box that is accessible from the surface.
   - Mark cleanout locations with survey flagging or paint for post-backfill identification.

3. Verify cleanout **cap and body** are appropriate material (PVC, same schedule as pipe, or approved equivalent).

![Building sewer cleanout at foundation exit, extending to grade](images/303_cleanout_at_building.jpg)

### Step 5: Verify Backwater Valve (If Required)

1. Check plans and permit for backwater valve requirement (FPC 715.1):
   - Required when any fixture drain is below the flood level rim of the next upstream manhole or when specified by the AHJ.
   - Common in Florida for systems where septic tank backup could flood fixtures.

2. If a backwater valve is present:
   - Verify it is installed on the building sewer **downstream of the last building drain connection**.
   - Confirm correct orientation (flow arrow pointing toward septic tank).
   - Valve body must be accessible after backfill (valve box or riser to grade).
   - Confirm the valve is listed and labeled (NSF/ANSI or ASSE standard).

3. If plans require a backwater valve and it is not installed = **HOLD** until installed.

### Step 6: Verify Septic Tank Installation

1. **Tank material and condition:**
   - Confirm tank material matches the FDOH permit (precast concrete per ASTM C1227 is most common in Florida; fiberglass or polyethylene also permitted).
   - Inspect the tank exterior (visible portion) for cracks, spalling, displacement, or damage from placement.
   - Check that all tank lids are present and structurally intact.
   - Verify the tank has **no visible holes, cracks through the wall, or separation at joints** (for two-piece tanks, check the mid-seam).

2. **Tank sizing:**
   - Confirm tank capacity matches the FDOH permit (typically based on number of bedrooms):
     - 1–2 bedrooms: minimum 900 gallons.
     - 3 bedrooms: minimum 1,050 gallons.
     - 4 bedrooms: minimum 1,250 gallons.
   - Verify the tank label or manufacturer stamp shows the rated capacity.

3. **Tank orientation and elevation:**
   - Inlet must be **higher than the outlet** (minimum 2 in. elevation differential between inlet and outlet invert, per FAC 64E-6.009).
   - Tank must be **level** (side-to-side). Use a 4 ft level across the top of the tank or across the lid openings.
   - Verify the tank top elevation allows for proper burial depth (typically 6–36 in. of cover over the top, per manufacturer and FDOH permit).

4. **Inlet and outlet baffles:**
   - Confirm the **inlet baffle (tee)** is present and properly oriented — tee extending downward into the tank, open at the bottom, directing flow downward to prevent short-circuiting.
   - Confirm the **outlet baffle (tee)** is present and extends downward into the tank to retain scum and solids. Outlet baffle tee should extend approximately 40% of the liquid depth below the waterline.
   - Baffles must be securely attached to the tank wall (not loose or displaced).

5. **Effluent filter (if required):**
   - Some FDOH permits and local AHJs require an effluent filter on the outlet baffle.
   - If required: verify the filter is installed, properly seated in the outlet tee, and accessible through the tank lid for future maintenance.

![Inlet baffle tee visible through access opening — flow direction downward](images/303_inlet_baffle_tee.jpg)

![Outlet baffle tee with effluent filter installed](images/303_outlet_baffle_filter.jpg)

### Step 7: Verify Septic Tank Watertightness

1. **Water test (most common for new installations):**
   - Fill the tank with water to the outlet invert elevation.
   - Allow to stand for a minimum of **24 hours** (or per FDOH/AHJ requirement).
   - Measure the water level before and after the hold period.
   - **PASS:** No measurable drop in water level (accounting for evaporation, which is negligible inside a closed tank over 24 hours).
   - **FAIL:** Measurable water level drop indicates a leak — tank must be repaired or replaced.

2. **Alternative vacuum test (per manufacturer):**
   - Some precast manufacturers provide vacuum test procedures. Follow manufacturer specs and FDOH guidance.

3. **Documentation:** Record test method, duration, water level at start and end, date/time, and licensed contractor name.

> **Note:** Many Florida AHJs and FDOH offices accept the manufacturer's water test certification for precast tanks delivered sealed. Confirm with the local FDOH office whether a field water test is required or if the manufacturer certification is sufficient.

### Step 8: Verify Connection — Building Sewer to Septic Tank Inlet

1. Confirm the building sewer pipe enters the septic tank through the **inlet opening** (not the outlet).
2. Verify the connection is:
   - Made with a **flexible coupling or approved adapter** that allows for differential settlement between the pipe and tank (rigid connections crack over time).
   - Sealed watertight — no visible gaps or light showing around the pipe at the tank wall.
   - Pipe enters the inlet tee/baffle and directs flow downward into the tank.

3. Verify the **invert elevation** of the building sewer at the tank inlet matches the plans and provides continuous downhill slope from the building.

![Building sewer pipe entering septic tank inlet with flexible coupling](images/303_sewer_to_tank_connection.jpg)

### Step 9: Verify Distribution Box (D-Box) — If Present

1. **D-box placement and level:**
   - D-box must be installed **level in all directions** (use a level across the top). This is critical — an unlevel D-box causes uneven distribution to drainfield laterals, which overloads one side and shortens drainfield life.
   - D-box must be set on a stable, compacted base (concrete pad or compacted aggregate) to prevent settling.

2. **Inlet connection:**
   - Confirm the pipe from the septic tank outlet connects to the D-box inlet.
   - Connection should be flexible (flexible coupling) to allow for differential settlement.

3. **Outlet connections:**
   - Confirm each D-box outlet connects to a separate drainfield lateral.
   - All outlet inverts must be **at the same elevation** (this is what distributes effluent equally).
   - Verify the number of outlets matches the number of drainfield laterals per plans.

4. **Speed levelers (if installed):**
   - Some D-boxes have adjustable speed leveler inserts on the outlets. If present, verify they are set to the same height per the contractor's level check.

5. **Structural condition:** No cracks, damage, or misalignment from placement.

![Distribution box level check — level across top showing even setting](images/303_dbox_level_check.jpg)

![D-box interior showing equal outlet inverts and connections to drainfield laterals](images/303_dbox_interior_outlets.jpg)

### Step 10: Verify Drainfield (Absorption Field) Construction

1. **Trench dimensions (per FDOH permit and FAC 64E-6.008):**
   - Trench width: typically 12–36 in. (per permit design).
   - Trench depth: per permit — must maintain minimum separation from seasonal high water table (SHWT).
   - Trench length: per permit design (based on soil percolation rate and daily flow).
   - Measure and confirm trench width, depth, and length at multiple points.

2. **Separation from SHWT:**
   - **Critical requirement:** Bottom of the drainfield aggregate must be a minimum of **24 in. above the estimated seasonal high water table (SHWT)** per FAC 64E-6.
   - Verify the drainfield elevation relative to the SHWT elevation documented in the FDOH soil evaluation report.
   - If groundwater is visible in the trench during inspection = **STOP WORK** — possible SHWT conflict. Notify FDOH immediately.

3. **Aggregate bedding:**
   - Drainfield aggregate (typically 3/4 in. to 2-1/2 in. washed rock or approved gravel) must be:
     - Clean, washed, free of fines (no sand or dirt mixed in).
     - Minimum 6 in. depth below the perforated pipe.
     - Minimum 2 in. depth above the perforated pipe (or per permit).
   - Aggregate extends the full width and length of the trench.

4. **Perforated pipe:**
   - Confirm perforated pipe is 4 in. diameter (typical) and laid **holes down** (perforations facing downward to distribute effluent into the aggregate, not upward where they clog with biomat).
   - Pipe must be level or have a very slight slope (no more than 1/16 in. per foot — drainfield pipe is nearly flat to distribute effluent evenly).
   - No sags, bellies, or backslope in the perforated pipe.

5. **Filter fabric / geotextile:**
   - If required by permit or standard practice: filter fabric is placed over the aggregate before backfill to prevent soil migration into the aggregate (extends over the top and sides of the aggregate layer).
   - Verify fabric is non-woven geotextile, not plastic sheeting.

6. **Trench spacing:**
   - Minimum 6 ft center-to-center between parallel drainfield trenches (per FAC 64E-6.008).
   - Measure and confirm.

![Perforated pipe laid on aggregate bed — holes facing down](images/303_perforated_pipe_holes_down.jpg)

![Filter fabric placed over aggregate before backfill](images/303_filter_fabric_over_aggregate.jpg)

### Step 11: Verify Connection — Septic Tank to D-Box to Drainfield

1. Trace the entire effluent path:
   - Septic tank outlet → pipe → D-box inlet (or directly to drainfield header if no D-box).
   - D-box outlets → individual drainfield laterals.

2. Verify all connections are:
   - Properly fitted (solvent-weld with primer, or gasketed).
   - Watertight.
   - Supported on bedding (not suspended or hanging).

3. Verify the **pipe slope** from septic tank outlet to D-box:
   - Minimum 1/8 in. per foot slope (gravity flow).
   - Continuous downhill — no bellies or sags.

4. Verify no **cross-connections** between drainfield laterals (each lateral must be independently fed from the D-box).

### Step 12: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire system from the building foundation to the farthest drainfield trench and confirm:
   - All piping routes match plans and FDOH permit.
   - All components (tank, D-box, drainfield) are properly installed and at correct elevations.
   - All setback distances verified and documented.
   - All connections are secure, sealed, and properly oriented.
   - Tank watertightness test has passed (or manufacturer certification accepted).
   - All deficiencies noted in earlier steps have been corrected.

2. Photograph the complete system per the Field Photo Checklist below.

3. Record on the field inspection form:
   - All measurements (pipe sizes, slopes, setback distances, trench dimensions, aggregate depths).
   - Tank capacity, material, and watertightness test results.
   - D-box leveling confirmation.
   - Drainfield construction verification (trench dimensions, aggregate, pipe orientation, SHWT separation).
   - Any corrections made or required.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All setback distances meet or exceed FDOH permit requirements; building sewer properly sized (4 in. min), sloped (1/8 in./ft min), and bedded with long-turn fittings only; all joints show purple primer (PVC) or proper gasket seating (SDR 35); cleanouts at required locations and accessible; septic tank properly sized per bedroom count, level, inlet higher than outlet, baffles present and secure, watertightness test passes; D-box level with equal outlet inverts; drainfield trenches at design dimensions with clean aggregate, perforated pipe holes-down and level, minimum 24 in. above SHWT; filter fabric in place; all connections watertight and properly oriented |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiency correctable on-site before backfill: one cleanout cap missing, D-box slightly off-level (correctable with shim), minor slope adjustment on one pipe segment, filter fabric not yet placed but aggregate and pipe verified, effluent filter not yet installed in outlet baffle but available on-site |
| **FAIL** | Setback distance violation (any component closer to well, property line, or water body than permitted); building sewer undersized or wrong material; pressure test or tank watertightness test failure; septic tank cracked, damaged, or wrong capacity; inlet not higher than outlet; baffles missing or displaced; D-box significantly off-level (>1/4 in. across top); drainfield aggregate contaminated with fines; perforated pipe installed holes-up; drainfield trench bottom below 24 in. above SHWT; groundwater visible in drainfield trench; cross-connections between drainfield laterals |

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Confuses municipal sewer requirements with septic system requirements.** LLMs frequently apply municipal sewer tap and lateral standards (manholes, service saddles, invert elevations at the main) to septic system inspections. Septic systems are governed by FAC 64E-6, not by the municipal utility standards. The permitting authority is FDOH (Florida Department of Health), not the utility company. AI-generated checklists that reference "manhole invert" or "service saddle" for a septic project are applying the wrong standard entirely.

2. **Gets drainfield perforated pipe orientation wrong.** AI frequently states perforated pipe should be installed "holes up" to allow effluent to rise and distribute. This is **incorrect for conventional gravity drainfields in Florida.** Perforations face **downward** so effluent drains into the aggregate bed and infiltrates the soil. Holes-up orientation causes the pipe to fill and overflow from the top, creating channeling and uneven distribution. This single error can cause premature drainfield failure.

3. **Omits the SHWT separation requirement.** The 24 in. minimum separation between the bottom of the drainfield aggregate and the seasonal high water table (SHWT) is the most critical design constraint for Florida septic systems. AI responses routinely discuss drainfield trench depth without referencing SHWT at all, or confuse SHWT with the current water table reading. SHWT is the **highest estimated** groundwater level during the wet season, documented in the soil evaluation report — not the level observed on the day of inspection.

4. **Treats the distribution box as optional or decorative.** When a D-box is part of the permitted design, its **level setting is the single most important field verification** for drainfield longevity. An unlevel D-box sends more effluent to one lateral than the others, overloading that section of drainfield and causing premature failure. AI checklists often list "verify D-box" without specifying that it must be level in all directions and that all outlet inverts must be at the same elevation.

5. **Applies building drain slope minimums to drainfield laterals.** AI often states drainfield perforated pipe should be sloped at 1/4 in. per foot (the building drain standard). Drainfield laterals are designed to distribute effluent evenly and must be **nearly level** — maximum slope of approximately 1/16 in. per foot. Excessive slope causes all effluent to discharge at the far end of the lateral, defeating the purpose of the drainfield.

## 10) Documentation Minimum

- [ ] Project address, permit number (building permit AND FDOH septic permit), date, time, weather conditions.
- [ ] Name and license number of inspecting engineer, inspector, or licensed septic contractor.
- [ ] FDOH permit reference (permit number, approved date, approved system size and type).
- [ ] Plan sheet reference (plumbing plan revision, site plan revision, FDOH-approved site plan).
- [ ] Setback distance measurements: well, property lines, structures, water bodies, pools, stormwater.
- [ ] Building sewer verification: material (Schedule 40 or SDR 35), pipe size, slope measurements (minimum 3 locations), fitting types, joint integrity (primer/gasket).
- [ ] Cleanout locations and accessibility.
- [ ] Backwater valve installation (if required): location, orientation, accessibility.
- [ ] Septic tank verification: material, capacity (gallons), manufacturer/label, level check, inlet/outlet elevation differential, baffle presence and condition, effluent filter (if required).
- [ ] Tank watertightness test results: test method, duration, water level at start and end, pass/fail.
- [ ] Distribution box (if present): level check, inlet/outlet connections, outlet invert equality, structural condition.
- [ ] Drainfield verification: trench width, depth, length, aggregate type and depth (below and above pipe), perforated pipe diameter, pipe orientation (holes-down), pipe slope (near-level), filter fabric presence, trench spacing.
- [ ] SHWT separation confirmation: drainfield aggregate bottom elevation vs. SHWT per soil evaluation report.
- [ ] Connection verification: building sewer to tank inlet (flexible coupling, watertight), tank outlet to D-box, D-box to drainfield laterals.
- [ ] Photo set with scale reference (minimum 18 photos per inspection — see Field Photo Checklist).
- [ ] Deficiency list (if any) with required corrective actions and completion verification.
- [ ] Final disposition: PASS / HOLD / FAIL with inspecting authority signature and date.
- [ ] **Limitations statement:** "This inspection is limited to visual observation and measurement of accessible components at the time of inspection. Soil conditions, SHWT elevation, and system sizing are based on the FDOH-approved soil evaluation and permit. Concealed conditions after backfill are excluded from the scope of this inspection."

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Setback distance violation (any component) | **FAIL / STOP WORK** → Notify FDOH; system may require redesign and new permit |
| Building sewer pressure test failure or visible leak | **FAIL** → Locate and repair leak; retest before backfill |
| Septic tank cracked, damaged, or leaking (watertightness test fails) | **FAIL** → Tank must be repaired (if repairable per manufacturer) or replaced; retest |
| Septic tank wrong capacity for permitted bedroom count | **FAIL / STOP WORK** → Requires tank replacement and possible FDOH permit amendment |
| Inlet/outlet elevation reversed or inlet lower than outlet | **FAIL** → Tank must be reset or replaced at correct orientation |
| Baffles missing, broken, or displaced | **FAIL** → Baffles must be repaired or replaced before the system is placed in service |
| D-box significantly off-level (>1/4 in. across top) | **HOLD** → Contractor must re-level D-box on compacted base before backfill |
| Drainfield aggregate contaminated with fines or soil | **FAIL** → Remove contaminated aggregate; replace with clean washed rock |
| Perforated pipe installed holes-up | **FAIL** → Pipe must be rotated to holes-down orientation |
| Drainfield trench bottom less than 24 in. above SHWT | **FAIL / STOP WORK** → Notify FDOH; system may require mound modification or redesign |
| Groundwater visible in drainfield trench at time of inspection | **STOP WORK** → Possible SHWT conflict; notify FDOH and septic designer; do not backfill |
| Building sewer uses short-turn 90° fittings in horizontal run | **FAIL** → Replace with long-turn fittings |
| Missing cleanout at building or at required interval | **HOLD** → Install cleanout before backfill |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Site overview** — Wide shot showing the relationship between the building, septic tank, D-box, and drainfield area. Include property markers or reference points.
2. **Setback measurement** — Tape/wheel showing distance from septic tank or drainfield to the nearest well, property line, or water body.
3. **Building sewer in trench** — Pipe visible in trench showing slope direction, pipe markings (material, size), and bedding.
4. **Building sewer fitting** — Close-up of long-sweep fitting at a change of direction.
5. **Sewer joint detail** — Close-up of PVC joint with purple primer, or SDR 35 gasketed joint showing full insertion.
6. **Cleanout at building** — Cleanout body and cap at the foundation exit, showing accessibility.
7. **Backwater valve** — If present: valve body, flow arrow, and access box/riser.
8. **Septic tank exterior** — Tank in excavation showing condition, lids, and surrounding soil.
9. **Tank level check** — Level placed across the tank top or lid openings.
10. **Tank inlet baffle** — View through access opening showing inlet tee directing flow downward.
11. **Tank outlet baffle** — View through access opening showing outlet tee and effluent filter (if installed).
12. **Tank capacity label** — Manufacturer stamp or label showing rated gallon capacity.
13. **Building sewer to tank connection** — Pipe entering tank inlet with flexible coupling visible.
14. **D-box level check** — Level across D-box top showing even setting (if D-box is present).
15. **D-box interior** — View inside D-box showing outlet inverts at equal elevation.
16. **Drainfield trench cross-section** — Trench showing aggregate depth (below and above pipe), trench width, and perforated pipe.
17. **Perforated pipe orientation** — Close-up confirming holes face downward.
18. **Filter fabric** — Geotextile placed over aggregate before backfill.
19. **Drainfield overview** — Wide shot showing all trenches, spacing, and connection to D-box.
20. **Any deficiencies** — Close-up photographs of any issues found with clear identification and measurement reference.
