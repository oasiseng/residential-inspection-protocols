# Protocol 301: Plumbing 1st Rough (Underground) — Pre-Backfill Verification

> **Inspection Type:** Plumbing — DWV & Water Supply Underground
> **Version:** 1.0
> **Jurisdiction Basis:** Florida Plumbing Code (FPC), based on Florida-amended International Plumbing Code; Florida Building Code (FBC), 8th Edition — Plumbing Volume
> **Author/Reviewer:** [Oasis Engineering]
> **Revision Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## 1) Scope

Pre-backfill verification of under-slab plumbing rough-in for single-family residential construction. This protocol covers the inspection window **after trenching and pipe installation but before backfill and concrete slab placement**.

### In Scope
- Drain, Waste, Vent (DWV) piping material, sizing, slope, and fittings (underground).
- Water supply piping (hot and cold lines) material, sizing, fittings, and stub-out locations.
- Solvent-weld joint integrity (purple primer application visible on PVC).
- Cleanout locations and accessibility.
- Trap arm lengths and vent connections.
- Building sewer connection and backwater valve (if required).
- Pressure testing (water supply and DWV).
- Trench conditions, bedding, cover, and separation between water and sewer lines.
- Toilet flange hub height above finished floor elevation.

### Out of Scope
- Final trap testing or air admittance valve (AAV) approval (approved at final rough or trim-out).
- Pressure regulator settings or backflow device certification (verified at final inspection).
- Above-slab plumbing rough-in or final fixture connections.
- Existing or off-site utilities (water service from meter; sewer service to municipal connection is visual only).

---

## 2) Objective

Determine whether the under-slab plumbing rough-in is ready for backfill and slab placement by verifying:
- **PASS:** All piping, fittings, slope, sizing, joints, and pressure testing meet plans and FPC.
- **HOLD:** Minor corrections needed before backfill authorization (e.g., joint touch-up, minor slope adjustment).
- **FAIL / STOP WORK:** Critical deficiencies requiring repair, replacement, or re-pressure testing before backfill.

## 5) Safety + Non-Destructive Limits

- **Trench safety:** Do not enter excavations deeper than 4 ft without shoring or sloping per OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart P. Inspect from the edge where possible; use a probe rod or stooping position to observe.
- **Do not disturb placed piping** during inspection — no unauthorized moving, bending, or pressure application beyond the approved test protocol.
- **Pressure testing protocol:** Only hydrostatic (water) or low-pressure air test (≤5 PSI for DWV) performed by licensed plumber per FPC. Inspector witnesses and observes gauge continuously during test period. **Do not exceed 5 PSI for DWV air test** — risk of line rupture.
- **Stop condition:** If trenches show active groundwater, unstable walls, or evidence of utility conflicts — STOP and notify the contractor and plumbing engineer before proceeding.
- **Do not apply force** to test any joint or fitting during visual inspection.

## 7) Procedure Steps

### Step 1: Verify Piping Material and Plans Compliance

1. Walk the entire under-slab piping route and confirm the following per plans:
   - **DWV piping:** Schedule 40 PVC (ASTM D2665) — no PVC pressure-rated pipe in DWV system.
   - **Water supply:** Copper (Type L or Type K per ASTM B88) OR PEX (ASTM F876) per plans. Confirm consistency (do not mix materials without dielectric unions).
   - Identify and mark all stub-out locations (laundry, kitchen, bathrooms, etc.) per plans.

2. Check that **no short-turn (90-degree) fittings** are used in horizontal DWV piping.
   - Only long-turn (long-sweep) fittings are permitted for changes of direction in horizontal drainage.
   - Verify fittings at each change of direction; photograph any violations.

3. Verify no **S-traps, double-trapping, or crown venting** is present:
   - S-trap (trap outlet rises above the trap inlet) — not permitted under FPC.
   - Double-trap (two traps in series for the same fixture) — not permitted.
   - Crown vent (vent connection at the crown or top of the trap) — not permitted; vent must be taken off above the centerline.

### Step 2: Verify DWV Pipe Sizing and Slope

1. Verify **pipe sizing** matches plans and fixture unit calculations:
   - 1.5 in. for lavatory, 2 in. for kitchen/bath/toilet waste, 3 in. or 4 in. for main stack (per FPC Table).
   - Confirm stack size at slab level matches plans.

2. Check **minimum slope** using a 2–4 ft level:
   - **Pipes ≤3 in. diameter:** Minimum 1/4 in. per foot slope.
   - **Pipes ≥4 in. diameter:** Minimum 1/8 in. per foot slope.
   - Measure slope at a minimum of 3 locations along each horizontal run.
   - Place level on pipe and measure rise/run; confirm slope is downward toward the stack or cleanout.

3. **Red flag:** Pipes with insufficient slope (sag) or backslope (rising toward the stack) — HOLD for correction.

### Step 3: Verify Cleanout Locations and Accessibility

1. Confirm cleanouts are installed per FPC requirements:
   - Cleanout at the base of each stack (where DWV enters the building sewer).
   - Cleanout at each change of direction >45 degrees in horizontal runs.
   - Cleanout at intervals not exceeding 100 ft of horizontal pipe length.

2. Verify cleanout plugs are **accessible after slab pour**:
   - Cleanout location should be marked (paint or flagging) so it is not buried under the slab.
   - Top of cleanout should be set slightly above the planned slab elevation (to be cut and capped after slab pour).

3. Check that cleanout **body and cap are appropriate Schedule 40 PVC** fitting (not drainage-only ABS or mismatched material).

### Step 4: Verify Trap Arm Lengths and Vent Connections

1. Measure **trap arm length** (distance from trap outlet to vent connection) at each fixture location:
   - Consult FPC Table 1002.1 for maximum trap arm length based on pipe size.
   - Typical: 2 in. trap arm = 5 ft maximum distance to vent.
   - Measure with tape; confirm distance does not exceed table limit.

2. Verify **vent takeoff location:**
   - Vent connection must be taken off **above the centerline** of the horizontal drainage pipe (not at the crown or below centerline).
   - Vent must rise above flood level rim (minimum 6 in. above highest fixture rim in the home, per FPC).
   - Vent pipes must be sized per plans and FPC Table.

3. Check for **no crown venting** (vent connection at the top of the pipe) — vent must be above centerline with a 45-degree or steeper takeoff toward vertical.

### Step 5: Verify Building Sewer Connection

1. Locate the point where the under-slab DWV system connects to the building sewer (typically at the foundation perimeter or exterior wall):
   - Confirm connection is made with appropriate Schedule 40 PVC fitting (long-turn fitting).
   - Verify the building sewer is pitched toward the public sanitary sewer or septic system.

2. **If a backwater valve is required per plans or local code:**
   - Verify it is installed on the building sewer side (downstream of the building drain).
   - Check that the valve body is installed per manufacturer instructions (orientation).
   - Confirm the valve is accessible for future maintenance (not buried under a concrete pad).

3. Measure and confirm the sewer line slope toward the point of disposal.

### Step 6: Verify Solvent-Weld Joint Integrity (Purple Primer)

1. **Visual inspection of all PVC solvent-weld joints** (this is Florida-specific and critical):
   - All joints must show **visible purple primer** on the outside of the fitting socket and on the pipe exterior.
   - Purple primer is required by Florida Plumbing Code and is the primary evidence that joints were properly primed before cementing.
   - Photograph joints showing purple primer as evidence of compliance.

2. Check that **joint surfaces are smooth and properly seated:**
   - No gaps between pipe and fitting (pipe should be fully inserted into fitting socket).
   - No visible cement drip or overflow that obscures the joint.
   - No cracks or separation at the joint.

3. **Red flag:** Joints without visible purple primer or joints that appear to be dry (no primer/cement visible) — HOLD for touch-up by licensed plumber.

### Step 7: Verify Water Supply Piping (Hot and Cold)

1. Trace both **hot and cold water supply lines** from the service entry point:
   - Confirm material per plans (copper or PEX).
   - Identify all hot and cold line stubs per fixture locations (laundry, kitchen, bathrooms, hose bib, etc.).

2. Verify **proper fittings and no dissimilar metal contact:**
   - If copper and PEX are both present, confirm dielectric unions are used at any connection point (no direct copper-to-PEX contact).
   - No galvanized steel in water supply (corrosion risk in Florida).

3. Check that hot and cold lines are **properly identified and separated:**
   - Lines should be labeled with tape or paint (H for hot, C for cold) if not visually obvious by routing.
   - Hot and cold should run parallel without contact (no thermal transfer).

4. Verify **stub-out locations match plans:**
   - Kitchen sink (hot and cold).
   - Lavatory (hot and cold).
   - Shower/tub (hot and cold).
   - Toilet (cold only).
   - Laundry (hot and cold).
   - Hose bib (typically cold).

5. Check that all **stub-out caps are in place** (plastic or brass caps on open pipe ends to prevent debris ingress during construction).

### Step 8: Verify Trench Conditions, Bedding, and Pipe Support

1. Inspect the **trench bottom** along the entire piping run:
   - Bottom should be free of rocks, debris, or sharp objects.
   - Piping should rest on **clean fill or sand bedding**, not directly on rocky or uneven soil.
   - No visible roots, organic matter, or construction debris touching the pipe.

2. Verify **minimum burial depth** and cover over piping:
   - Minimum 12 in. of cover over any underground piping per FPC (measured from top of pipe to finished grade or slab surface).
   - Piping should not be exposed or at risk of damage during backfill.

3. Check **separation between water and sewer lines** (if both are in the same trench):
   - Minimum **12 in. vertical separation** between water supply and DWV/building sewer (per FPC).
   - **Water line must be above sewer line** (gravity requires sewer to be lower).
   - No direct contact between lines.

4. Verify **no crossing utilities** or conflicts:
   - Confirm water supply line does not rest on or contact electrical conduit (minimum 12 in. separation preferred).
   - No gas lines in contact with water or sewer piping.

### Step 9: Verify Toilet Flange Elevation (Hub Height)

1. Locate all **toilet waste outlet flanges** set into the slab:
   - Flange hub (the opening where the toilet wax ring sits) should be set at the planned **finished floor elevation + finished floor thickness**.
   - Typical: If finished floor is 3/4 in. tile over concrete, hub should be 3/4 in. above the slab top (so finished floor surface is at the hub edge).

2. Measure from the planned slab top to the hub:
   - Use a height stick or story pole to mark the planned slab elevation.
   - Verify hub sits at the correct height relative to planned floor finish.

3. **Red flag:** Flanges set too low (below finished floor) or too high (above finished floor) — HOLD for adjustment before slab pour.

### Step 10: Pressure Testing (DWV and Water Supply)

#### DWV Pressure Test (Water Test Method)
1. **DWV water test** (most common for under-slab rough):
   - All openings are capped and the system is filled with water to the top of the highest vent stack.
   - System must hold the water for a minimum of **15 minutes with no drop visible in the vent** (or per AHJ requirement).
   - Inspector must **witness the entire test period** with the gauge or water level visible throughout.
   - If any drop is observed: FAIL — system must be drained, the leak located and repaired, and the test repeated.

2. **Alternative DWV air test** (if specified):
   - Air pressure applied to system at **5 PSI maximum** (no higher — risk of rupture).
   - Gauge must be visible and stable for 15 minutes with no drop.
   - Inspector witnesses continuously.

#### Water Supply Pressure Test
1. System is pressurized with **80 PSI hydrostatic pressure** (or 1.5x normal working pressure per AHJ).
2. Test duration: **15 minutes minimum** with no visible drop on the gauge.
3. Gauge must be accurate to ±0.5 PSI and **must remain visible and readable throughout the test period**.
4. Inspector witnesses the entire test.
5. **PASS:** No drop in pressure after 15 minutes.
6. **FAIL:** Any drop = system has a leak; must be repaired and retested.

#### Documentation of Test Results
- Record test type (water or air), starting pressure, ending pressure, test duration, date/time, and licensed plumber name.
- Photograph the gauge during the test (with clearly visible reading).
- Any failed test must be documented with corrective actions and re-test results.

### Step 11: Final Walkthrough and Documentation

1. Walk the entire under-slab plumbing system and confirm:
   - All piping routes match plans.
   - All junctions and fittings are secure and properly installed.
   - All pressure tests have passed.
   - All deficiencies noted in earlier steps have been corrected.

2. Photograph:
   - Overall view of trench and piping layout (wide shot showing slope).
   - DWV stack at slab penetration with cleanout visible.
   - Close-up of solvent-weld joint with purple primer visible (minimum 3 joints).
   - Water supply hot and cold line identification (labeled or routed).
   - Trap arm length measurement (tape measure).
   - Vent takeoff angle and location (showing above centerline).
   - Building sewer connection point.
   - Toilet flange hub height (height stick showing elevation).
   - Trench bedding and pipe support.
   - Water/sewer line separation (if applicable).
   - Pressure test gauge during test (clearly visible reading).
   - Any deficiencies or red flags encountered.

3. Record on field inspection form:
   - All measurements (pipe sizes, slopes, distances, heights).
   - Test results (pressure values, duration, pass/fail).
   - Material verification (DWV type, water supply type, fittings).
   - Cleanout locations and spacing.
   - Any corrections made or required.

## 8) Interpretation Criteria

| Result | Condition |
|---|---|
| **PASS** | All piping materials, sizes, and slopes meet plans and FPC; all joints show purple primer; pressure tests pass (no drop); cleanouts properly located; trap arm lengths and vent connections compliant; trench bedding and cover adequate; no S-traps, double-traps, or crown venting; flange heights correct |
| **HOLD** | Minor deficiencies correctable on-site before backfill (e.g., primer touch-up on 1–2 joints, minor slope adjustment, one stub-out cap missing, flange height adjustment possible before slab pour) |
| **FAIL** | Pressure test failure with identified leak; missing or undersized piping per plans; prohibited fittings (short-turn 90 in DWV); S-trap or crown venting discovered; joints without primer and no time to cure; insufficient trench cover or bedding; significant slope violation; building sewer connection defect; water/sewer separation violation; conflicting underground utilities |

## 9) What AI Gets Wrong

1. **Gets DWV slope minimums wrong.** LLMs frequently state 1/4 in. per foot for ALL horizontal DWV piping. In fact, FPC permits **1/8 in. per foot minimum for pipes 4 in. and larger**. Conversely, some AI systems allow insufficient slope on smaller pipes (2–3 in.), which causes stagnation. Always differentiate by pipe size and refer to FPC Chapter 7.

2. **Allows short-turn (90-degree) fittings in horizontal DWV.** This is a critical error. Horizontal drainage piping requires **long-turn (long-sweep) fittings only** to maintain velocity and prevent line obstruction. AI-generated checklists often omit this requirement entirely or confuse it with vent piping (where 90-degree fittings above the drainline are acceptable).

3. **Skips the purple primer visibility requirement.** Florida Plumbing Code mandates that all PVC solvent-weld joints receive purple-tinted primer before cementing, and the inspector must verify visible purple primer on the joint. This is a Florida-specific requirement that generic AI responses omit. Missing or undocumented primer is a code violation and a common field deficiency.

4. **Treats the pressure test as optional or cursory.** The pressure test is the **single most critical checkpoint** of the under-slab rough inspection. AI checklists often list "perform pressure test" as a single item without specifying test pressure, test duration (15 minutes), the requirement that the gauge remain visible and readable throughout, or that the inspector must witness continuously. A leak found after backfill requires cutting the slab.

5. **Ignores water/sewer line separation in shared trenches.** When water supply and sewer lines are run in the same trench, FPC requires minimum **12 in. vertical separation with water above sewer**. AI plumbing guides rarely mention this requirement and sometimes suggest "co-location" of utilities without regard to separation or elevation. Violation risks contamination in case of sewer line rupture.

## 11) Escalation Path

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Pressure test failure (any drop during 15-minute hold) | FAIL → Require immediate leak location and repair; re-test before backfill |
| Purple primer not visible on solvent-weld joints | HOLD → Require inspection of joint cure; touch-up with primer and cement by licensed plumber; document cure time (24 hr minimum) |
| S-trap or double-trap discovered | FAIL → Require piping reconfiguration; violates FPC Chapter 10 |
| Crown venting (vent at top of pipe, not above centerline) | FAIL → Require vent takeoff relocation to above centerline position |
| Short-turn (90-degree) fittings in horizontal DWV | FAIL → Require fitting replacement with long-turn fitting |
| Slope insufficient (<1/4 in./ft for ≤3 in. pipe, <1/8 in./ft for ≥4 in. pipe) | HOLD → Trench adjustment required before backfill (piping re-sloped or slab adjusted) |
| Insufficient trench cover (<12 in. over piping) | HOLD → Adjust slab elevation or relocate piping to achieve minimum cover |
| Water/sewer line separation <12 in. or water line below sewer | FAIL → Require line relocation or trench reconstruction |
| Building sewer connection defect or missing backwater valve (if required) | HOLD → Require connection repair or valve installation before backfill |
| Cleanout inaccessible after slab pour (trapped under slab) | HOLD → Relocate cleanout to accessible location; adjust top elevation above finished floor |
| Toilet flange hub set below or significantly above planned floor | HOLD → Flange elevation adjustment required before slab pour |
| Conflicting underground utilities (water on electrical, gas line contact, etc.) | STOP WORK → Require utility locating and coordination with utility company and contractor |
| Trench water intrusion or groundwater at piping elevation | HOLD → Require dewatering or drainage solution before backfill |

## Field Photo Checklist

Use this shot list for consistent documentation:

1. **Wide shot** — Entire trench and under-slab piping layout showing DWV and water supply routes, slope visible.
2. **DWV stack** — Close-up of main stack at slab penetration point, showing cleanout location and pipe size.
3. **PVC solvent joint #1** — Close-up of joint with purple primer clearly visible on fitting and pipe exterior.
4. **PVC solvent joint #2** — Another joint location showing primer (minimum 3 joints total throughout inspection).
5. **PVC solvent joint #3** — Third joint showing primer consistency across system.
6. **Water supply identification** — Hot and cold lines with labels/tape visible, showing routing.
7. **Trap arm length** — Tape measure showing distance from trap outlet to vent connection.
8. **Vent takeoff** — Close-up of vent connection location (showing it is above centerline, not at crown).
9. **Building sewer connection** — Point where under-slab DWV connects to building sewer; backwater valve (if present).
10. **Toilet flange hub** — Height stick or ruler showing hub elevation relative to planned slab surface.
11. **Trench bedding** — Bottom of trench showing clean fill, no rocks or debris against piping.
12. **Pipe support** — Supports or strapping keeping piping in correct position within trench.
13. **Water/sewer separation** — Vertical distance between lines (if co-located in same trench); tape measure showing distance.
14. **Cleanout locations** — Visible cleanout body and cap, showing accessibility.
15. **Pressure test gauge** — Photograph of gauge during test showing stable pressure reading (clearly readable, date/time stamp).
16. **Overview with context** — Property address marker, permit board, or address number in frame to confirm location.
